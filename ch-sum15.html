1. Classification, Isomerism & IUPAC Nomenclature

  • Hydrocarbons: Compounds of C & H only
  • Classification: Aliphatic (open chain) & Aromatic
  • Aliphatic → Saturated (alkanes), Unsaturated (alkenes, alkynes)
  • Alkanes: CnH₂n+₂, sp³, single bonds
  • Alkenes: CnH₂n, sp², one C=C
  • Alkynes: CnH₂n-₂, sp, one C≡C
  • Isomerism: Chain, position, functional, geometrical, optical (rare)
  • IUPAC: Longest chain, lowest number to multiple bond/functional group
  • Prefix for C=C: -ene, C≡C: -yne, multiple bonds: di, tri
  • JEE loves: IUPAC name of complex branched alkenes/alkynes

2. Alkanes – Conformations & Reactions

  • General formula: CnH₂n+₂, least reactive (sigma bonds only)
  • Conformations of ethane: Staggered (low energy), Eclipsed (high energy)
  • Newman projection: Staggered more stable by 12.6 kJ/mol
  • Preparation: Decarboxylation, Wurtz, hydrogenation of alkenes
  • Free radical halogenation: Chain reaction (Initiation → Propagation → Termination)
  • Reactivity order: 3° > 2° > 1° H (stability of radical)
  • Selectivity: Cl₂ (less selective), Br₂ (highly selective for 3°)
  • No geometrical isomerism in alkanes
  • Combustion: Complete → CO₂ + H₂O, Incomplete → CO or C
  • JEE favourite: Relative rates in chlorination/bromination

3. Alkenes – Structure & Reactions

  • General formula: CnH₂n, contain C=C (sp² carbon)
  • Geometrical isomerism: Cis & trans if each carbon has two different groups
  • Electrophilic addition: π bond acts as nucleophile
  • Markovnikov’s rule: H adds to carbon with more H
  • Anti-Markovnikov: Only HBr + peroxide (free radical mechanism)
  • Ozonolysis: Reductive → aldehydes, Oxidative → carboxylic acids
  • Addition reactions: H₂ (Ni), X₂, HX, H₂O/H⁺, cold alkaline KMnO₄ (syn dihydroxylation)
  • Polymerization: Ethene → Polyethene
  • Heat of hydrogenation ∝ stability (trans > cis > geminal)
  • JEE asks: Product of ozonolysis, major product with HX

4. Alkynes – Acidic Character & Reactions

  • General formula: CnH₂n-₂, contain C≡C (sp carbon)
  • Terminal alkynes are acidic: React with Na, NaNH₂, Ag₂O, Cu₂Cl₂
  • Acidity order: H–C≡C–H > R–C≡C–H > R–C≡C–R
  • Addition: Two-stage (first → alkene, then → alkane)
  • HX & H₂O follow Markovnikov, but trans addition with Br₂
  • With NaNH₂ → sodium alkynide (used for longer chain synthesis)
  • Ozonolysis → carboxylic acids
  • Hydroboration-oxidation → aldehyde (terminal alkyne)
  • Polymerization: Acetylene → benzene (cyclic trimer)
  • JEE loves: Test to distinguish terminal alkyne (Tollens, Fehling negative)

5. Aromatic Hydrocarbons – Benzene & Electrophilic Substitution

  • Benzene: C₆H₆, follows Hückel rule (4n+2 π electrons, n=1)
  • Resonance hybrid: Two Kekulé structures, bond length 139 pm
  • Aromaticity conditions: Cyclic, planar, conjugated, 4n+2 π electrons
  • Electrophilic aromatic substitution (ArSE): Rate-determining step is carbocation formation
  • Reactions: Halogenation (FeX₃), Nitration (HNO₃+H₂SO₄), Sulphonation (fuming H₂SO₄), FC alkylation/acylation
  • Ortho-para directors: –OH, –NH₂, –R, –X (halogens: o/p but deactivating)
  • Meta directors: –NO₂, –CN, –CHO, –COOH, –SO₃H
  • FC alkylation limitations: Rearrangement, polyalkylation
  • FC acylation: No rearrangement (acyl cation stable)
  • JEE favourite: Directive influence & major product in disubstituted benzene

6. JEE Must-Revise Points (High Weightage)

  • Markovnikov: H adds to C with more H; anti only HBr + peroxide
  • Ozonolysis: Reductive (Zn/H₂O) → aldehydes; Oxidative (H₂O₂) → acids
  • Terminal alkyne: Acidic, gives white ppt with AgNO₃/NH₃ & red ppt with Cu₂Cl₂/NH₃
  • Benzene does not decolourise Br₂ water or Baeyer’s reagent
  • o/p directors (except halogen) are activating; meta directors are deactivating
  • Stability of alkene: More substituted > trans > cis
  • Free radical chlorination: Relative rate 5 : 3.8 : 1 (3°:2°:1°)
  • Bromination highly selective for 3° hydrogen
  • Cis-alkene gives racemic diol, trans gives meso (symmetric)
  • Heat of combustion: More branched alkane → less heat (less stable)
