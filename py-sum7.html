1. Elasticity • Stress • Strain • Hooke’s Law

Hooke’s Law
Within elastic limit: Stress ∝ Strain → Stress = E × Strain
Y (Young’s) > B (Bulk) > η (Rigidity)

2. Viscosity • Terminal Velocity • Reynolds Number

Viscous force: F = −η A (dv/dx)
Terminal velocity: v_t = [2r²(ρ−σ)g]/(9η)
Reynolds Number (R_e)
R_e = ρ v D / η
• R_e < 1000 → Streamline flow
• R_e > 2000 → Turbulent flow

3. Bernoulli’s Theorem & Applications

Bernoulli’s Principle
P + ρgh + (1/2)ρv² = constant
Applications:
• Venturimeter
• Aspirator pump
• Blowing off roof during storm
• Magnus effect (spin of ball)
• Aerofoil (lift of aircraft)

4. Surface Tension • Capillary Rise • Angle of Contact

Surface Tension (T) = F/l Unit: N/m
Excess pressure in soap bubble: ΔP = 4T/R
Excess pressure in liquid drop: ΔP = 2T/R
Angle of Contact θShape of MeniscusCapillary Action
θ < 90° (Water-glass)ConcaveRise
θ > 90° (Mercury-glass)ConvexFall
θ = 90°FlatNo rise/fall
Capillary rise: h = (2T cosθ)/(ρ g r)

5. Heat • Temperature • Expansion • Specific Heat • Latent Heat

TypeCoefficientFormula
LinearαΔL = L₀ α ΔT
Areaβ = 2αΔA = A₀ β ΔT
Volumeγ = 3αΔV = V₀ γ ΔT
Specific heat: c = δQ/(m ΔT)
Molar specific heat: C = c × M
For ideal gas: C_p − C_v = R
γ = C_p / C_v
Latent heat of fusion (L_f) = 336 J/g (ice)
Latent heat of vaporisation (L_v) = 2260 J/g (water)

6. Newton’s Law of Cooling • Stefan’s Law • Black Body • Greenhouse Effect

Newton’s Law of Cooling
Rate of cooling ∝ (T − T₀) (valid when ΔT small)
Stefan-Boltzmann Law
Power radiated: P = σ A e T⁴ (σ = 5.67 × 10⁻⁸ Wm⁻²K⁻⁴)
Black Body: Perfect absorber & emitter (e = 1)
Wien’s Law: λ_max × T = constant
Greenhouse Effect: CO₂, CH₄ trap IR radiation → Earth warms
