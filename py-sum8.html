2. First Law of Thermodynamics

Statement

The total energy of an isolated system remains constant.
ΔU = Q − W  (Energy conservation applied to heat)

Internal energy (U) is a state function
Q and W are path functions
Cyclic process: ∮dU = 0 → ∮Q = ∮W

Quick Revision – Must Remember

  • ΔU = Q − W (work by system)
  • Isothermal: ΔU=0 → Q=W
  • Adiabatic: Q=0 → ΔU=−W
  • First Law: Energy conservation
  • Second Law: η < 100%, Entropy increases
  • Carnot efficiency: 1 − T_cold/T_hot
  • Refrigerator COP = T_cold/(T_hot − T_cold)
  • Reversible → quasi-static + no friction
  • Free expansion of ideal gas: ΔT=0, ΔU=0, W=0, Q=0 but irreversible
  • ΔS_universe ≥ 0 (equality for reversible)
