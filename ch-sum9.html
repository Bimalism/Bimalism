1. Modern Periodic Law & Present Form

  • Modern Periodic Law: Physical & chemical properties are periodic function of atomic number
  • Long form: 18 groups, 7 periods
  • Period number = principal quantum number of valence shell
  • Group number = valence electrons (for s,p) or (n-1)d + ns electrons (for d-block)
  • f-block placed below (Lanthanoids & Actinoids)
  • H placed in group 1 (not 17) due to +1 oxidation state
  • Noble gases in group 18 (inert)
  • Diagonal relationship: Li–Mg, Be–Al, B–Si
  • JEE loves: Predict position of element with Z = 120
  • Total elements known: 118 (Oganesson)

2. s, p, d, f Block Elements

  • s-block: Groups 1 & 2 → highly reactive metals
  • p-block: Groups 13–18 → metals, non-metals, metalloids
  • d-block: Groups 3–12 → transition metals
  • f-block: Lanthanoids (Ce–Lu), Actinoids (Th–Lr)
  • s-block: ns¹⁻², p-block: ns²np¹⁻⁶
  • d-block: (n–1)d¹⁻¹⁰ ns¹⁻², f-block: (n–2)f¹⁻¹⁴ (n–1)d⁰⁻¹ ns²
  • Alkali metals: Most electropositive
  • Halogens: Most electronegative non-metals
  • JEE asks: General electronic configuration of d-block
  • Noble gases: ns²np⁶ (He: 1s²)

3. Periodic Trends: Atomic Radius

  • Down the group: Atomic radius increases (more shells)
  • Across period (L→R): Atomic radius decreases (Z_eff increases)
  • Noble gases have largest radius in period (van der Waals)
  • Covalent radius < metallic < van der Waals
  • Exception: Ga < Al due to d-orbital contraction
  • Transition metals: Slight decrease due to poor shielding
  • Lanthanoid contraction: Hf ≈ Zr
  • Order: Li > Be > B > C (anomaly due to half-filled p)
  • JEE favourite: Arrange in increasing atomic radius
  • Atomic radius of Fe < Co < Ni (unexpected)

4. Ionic Radius

  • Cation < parent atom < anion
  • Down group: Ionic radius increases
  • Across period: Cations decrease, anions decrease after
  • Isoelectronic species: Higher Z → smaller radius (Na⁺ < Mg²⁺ < Al³⁺)
  • Fe²⁺ > Fe³⁺ (less nuclear pull)
  • O²⁻ > F⁻ > Na⁺ > Mg²⁺ (isoelectronic)
  • For same charge, radius ∝ atomic number down group
  • Transition metal ions: M²⁺ > M³⁺
  • JEE loves: Arrange isoelectronic ions by size
  • Covalent character ↑ → ionic radius ↓ (Fajans)

5. Ionization Enthalpy (IE)

  • Down group: IE decreases (size ↑, shielding ↑)
  • Across period: IE generally increases (Z_eff ↑)
  • Exceptions: Be > B, N > O (half & fully filled stability)
  • IE₁ < IE₂ < IE₃ (successive)
  • Noble gases: Highest IE
  • Alkali metals: Lowest IE
  • IE₂ of Na >> IE₁ (removal from noble gas config)
  • Transition metals: Slight increase
  • JEE asks: Why IE of N > O?
  • IE decreases down group faster in p-block than s-block

6. Electron Gain Enthalpy (ΔₑgH) & Electron Affinity

  • Down group: Electron gain enthalpy becomes less negative
  • Across period: Becomes more negative (L→R)
  • Cl > F (small size of F → repulsion)
  • Noble gases: Positive (stable config)
  • Halogens: Most negative ΔₑgH
  • Be, Mg, N: Positive (stable config)
  • Electron affinity: Energy released on adding e⁻
  • ΔₑgH₂ always positive (repulsion)
  • JEE favourite: Cl has higher electron affinity than F
  • Order: Cl > F > Br > I

7. Electronegativity

  • Down group: Electronegativity decreases
  • Across period: Increases (L→R)
  • F: Most electronegative (4.0 Pauling)
  • Cs: Least electronegative
  • Noble gases: Not assigned (no compounds)
  • Diagonal relationship due to similar charge/radius
  • Fajans rule: Small cation + large anion → covalent
  • Polarizing power ∝ charge/size of cation
  • JEE asks: Order of electronegativity
  • O > N > C > B (exception)

8. Valence & Oxidation States

  • s-block: Fixed valence (1 or 2)
  • p-block: Variable (group no. or group no. – 2)
  • d-block: Variable oxidation states (due to ns & (n-1)d)
  • Maximum O.S. = group number (for p-block)
  • Mn: +2 to +7, highest variability
  • Sc: Only +3, Zn: Only +2 (stable config)
  • Oxidizing power: Higher O.S. more oxidizing
  • Metallic character ↓ L→R, ↑ down group
  • JEE loves: Highest O.S. of Mn, Cr, etc.
  • Acidic nature of oxides: Increases L→R

9. JEE Must-Revise Points (Daily)

  • Cl > F in electron affinity & ionic radius
  • N > O in IE, Be > B in IE
  • Atomic radius ↓ L→R, ↑ down group
  • IE ↑ L→R, ↓ down group
  • Fajans: Small highly charged cation polarizes anion
  • Lanthanoid contraction → Zr ≈ Hf
  • Diagonal relationship: Li–Mg, Be–Al
  • Noble gases have largest atomic radius in period
  • O²⁻ > F⁻ > Na⁺ > Mg²⁺ (isoelectronic)
  • Oxidation state of Mn in KMnO₄ = +7
