1. Photosynthesis – Basics & Pigments

  • Photosynthesis: 6CO₂ + 6H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ (in presence of light & chlorophyll)
  • Site: Chloroplast (grana – light reaction, stroma – dark reaction)
  • Main pigment: Chlorophyll-a (reaction centre), chlorophyll-b, carotenoids (accessory)
  • Chlorophyll-a is universal pigment, absorbs best in blue & red region
  • Carotenoids protect from photo-oxidation (xanthophyll, carotene)
  • Photosynthesis is oxidation-reduction process (water oxidised, CO₂ reduced)
  • Autotrophic nutrition – only green plants & few bacteria
  • Emerson effect: Two light systems – PS-I (P700), PS-II (P680)
  • Quantum yield: 1/8 (8 photons required for 1 O₂ molecule)
  • Photosynthesis occurs only in presence of light (except chemosynthetic bacteria)

2. Light Reaction & Chemiosmotic Hypothesis

  • Light reaction occurs in grana thylakoids
  • Non-cyclic photophosphorylation: PS-II → PS-I → NADP⁺ → NADPH + ATP
  • Cyclic photophosphorylation: Only PS-I → only ATP (no NADPH, no O₂)
  • Z-scheme: Electron flow from H₂O → PS-II → plastoquinone → cyt b6f → plastocyanin → PS-I → ferredoxin → NADP⁺
  • O₂ evolution from water splitting (photolysis) by OEC in PS-II
  • Chemiosmotic hypothesis (Peter Mitchell): Proton gradient across thylakoid membrane drives ATP synthesis
  • ATP synthase (CF₀-CF₁) allows H⁺ flow to form ATP
  • Non-cyclic produces ATP + NADPH + O₂
  • Cyclic produces only ATP (in sulphur bacteria)
  • Light reaction products: ATP, NADPH, O₂

3. Dark Reaction – C3, C4 & Photorespiration

  • Dark reaction (Calvin cycle) occurs in stroma, light independent
  • C3 pathway: First stable product PGA (3-carbon), RuBP + CO₂ → 2PGA (by RuBisCO)
  • C4 pathway (Hatch-Slack): First product OAA (4-carbon), in mesophyll → bundle sheath
  • Kranz anatomy in C4 plants (bundle sheath with large chloroplasts)
  • C4 plants: Maize, sugarcane – no photorespiration, high temperature optimum
  • Photorespiration: RuBisCO acts as oxygenase → 2PGA → no ATP/NADPH, waste process
  • C3 plants show photorespiration, C4 plants minimise it
  • CAM pathway: Night CO₂ fixation (malic acid), day decarboxylation (Crassulaceae)
  • Law of limiting factors (Blackman): Rate limited by scarcest factor
  • Calvin cycle: 6 turns → 1 glucose (18 ATP + 12 NADPH)

4. Factors Affecting Photosynthesis

  • Light intensity: Rate increases linearly then saturates
  • CO₂ concentration: Limiting below 0.03%, saturation at 0.1–1%
  • Temperature: Optimum 25–35°C (enzyme controlled)
  • Water: Indirect effect via stomatal closure
  • O₂ concentration: High O₂ increases photorespiration
  • Chlorophyll content: Directly proportional
  • Blackman’s law of limiting factors applicable
  • C4 plants have higher temperature & light saturation point
  • CO₂ compensation point: Zero photosynthesis (C3: 25–100 ppm, C4: 0–10 ppm)
  • Internal factors: Leaf age, orientation, anatomy

5. Respiration – Glycolysis & Fermentation

  • Respiration: C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + 38 ATP
  • Glycolysis (EMP pathway): Cytoplasm, glucose → 2 pyruvate + 2 ATP + 2 NADH
  • Anaerobic respiration: No O₂ → fermentation
  • Alcoholic fermentation (yeast): Pyruvate → ethanol + CO₂
  • Lactic fermentation (muscle): Pyruvate → lactate
  • Glycolysis common to aerobic & anaerobic
  • Net gain in anaerobic: Only 2 ATP per glucose
  • Pasteur effect: Aerobic condition suppresses fermentation
  • Glycolysis: 10 enzyme-catalysed steps
  • Fructose-1,6-bisphosphate split into 2 triose phosphates

6. Aerobic Respiration – TCA & ETS

  • Pyruvate → Acetyl CoA (link reaction) + CO₂ + NADH
  • TCA/Krebs cycle: Mitochondria, Acetyl CoA → 2CO₂ + 3NADH + 1FADH₂ + 1ATP
  • Total from one glucose: 8 NADH, 2 FADH₂, 2 ATP (substrate level)
  • ETS: Inner mitochondrial membrane, NADH → 3 ATP, FADH₂ → 2 ATP
  • Total ATP: Glycolysis 8 + Link 6 + TCA 24 + 2 = 38 (or 36 in eukaryotes)
  • Oxidative phosphorylation via chemiosmosis
  • Cytochromes: b, c, a, a₃ (complex IV)
  • Complex I, III, IV pump H⁺ → proton gradient
  • ATP synthase uses proton flow
  • Krebs cycle discovered by Hans Krebs

7. Respiratory Quotient & Amphibolic Pathway

  • RQ = CO₂ produced / O₂ consumed
  • Carbohydrates: RQ = 1, Fats: RQ < 1 (0.7), Proteins: RQ ≈ 0.9
  • Succulents (night acid): RQ = 0 (no CO₂ release)
  • Anaerobic: RQ = ∞ (no O₂ consumed)
  • Amphibolic pathway: Respiration intermediates used for synthesis (e.g., acetyl CoA → fatty acids)
  • Respiratory substrates: Carbohydrates → fats → proteins
  • Fat respiration requires extra O₂ → RQ < 1
  • Germinating oily seeds have RQ < 1
  • RQ indicates type of substrate being respired
  • Amphibolic: Both catabolic and anabolic

8. Plant Growth & Growth Curve

  • Growth: Irreversible increase in size, meristematic activity
  • Phases of growth: Lag → Log (exponential) → Stationary
  • Sigmoid growth curve typical for limited resources
  • Arithmetic growth: Only one daughter cell divides (root elongation)
  • Geometric growth: Both daughter cells divide
  • Growth rate = Increase in growth / time
  • Conditions: Water, oxygen, nutrients, optimum temperature, light
  • Differentiation: Cells mature to perform specific function
  • Dedifferentiation: Mature cells regain meristematic nature (cork cambium)
  • Redifferentiation: Dedifferentiated cells mature again

9. Growth Regulators – Auxin, Gibberellin, Cytokinin

  • Auxin (IAA): Apical dominance, phototropism, root initiation, parthenocarpy
  • Gibberellin: Stem elongation, seed germination (α-amylase synthesis), bolting
  • Cytokinin (zeatin, kinetin): Cell division, delay senescence (Richmond-Lang effect)
  • Auxin + cytokinin ratio controls differentiation (high auxin → root, high cytokinin → shoot)
  • Discovery: Went (auxin), Kurosawa (gibberellin), Miller (kinetin)
  • GA breaks seed dormancy in cereals
  • Cytokinin promotes lateral bud growth (breaks apical dominance)
  • Auxin promotes abscission of older leaves
  • Bakane disease (foolish seedling) caused by GA from fungus
  • Synthetic auxins: 2,4-D (weedicide), NAA

10. Ethylene & ABA + Vernalisation & Photoperiodism

  • Ethylene: Fruit ripening, promotes abscission, triple response in seedlings
  • ABA: Stress hormone, seed dormancy, stomatal closure, abscission
  • Photoperiodism: Response to duration of light (short-day, long-day, day-neutral plants)
  • SDP: Chrysanthemum, rice; LDP: Wheat, oat; DNP: Tomato, maize
  • Vernalisation: Promotion of flowering by cold treatment (winter wheat, biennials)
  • Phytochrome: Red (Pr) ↔ Far-red (Pfr) photoreceptor
  • Pfr promotes flowering in LDP, inhibits in SDP
  • Critical day length important for flowering
  • Florigen: Hypothetical flowering hormone
  • Ethylene most produced gaseous hormone
