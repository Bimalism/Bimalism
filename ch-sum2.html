1. Nature of Electromagnetic Radiation & Photoelectric Effect

  • EMR has dual nature: wave (λ, ν, c = νλ) & particle (photon E = hν)
  • Photoelectric effect: Light ejects electrons from metal surface
  • Threshold frequency ν₀: Minimum frequency required
  • Work function φ = hν₀ (energy needed to remove electron)
  • Kinetic energy of photoelectron: K.E.max = hν – hν₀ = h(ν – ν₀)
  • Stopping potential V₀: eV₀ = h(ν – ν₀)
  • Intensity ∝ number of photons, not energy of single photon
  • No time lag, instantaneous emission
  • Einstein got Nobel Prize 1921 for this
  • JEE loves graph of K.E. vs frequency & V₀ vs ν
  • Proved particle nature of light

2. Spectrum of Hydrogen Atom

  • Rydberg formula: 1/λ = R (1/n₁² – 1/n₂²)
  • R = 109677 cm⁻¹ (Rydberg constant)
  • Lyman series: UV region (n₁=1, n₂>1)
  • Balmer series: Visible (n₁=2, n₂>2)
  • Paschen: IR (n₁=3), Brackett (n₁=4), Pfund (n₁=5)
  • Maximum lines when electron jumps from n=∞ → n=1
  • Number of lines = (n₂ – n₁)(n₂ – n₁ + 1)/2
  • Spectral lines prove discrete energy levels
  • Explained by Bohr model
  • JEE asks: Wavelength of first line of Balmer series

3. Bohr Model of Hydrogen Atom – Postulates & Derivation

  • Postulate 1: Electrons revolve in stationary orbits (no energy loss)
  • Postulate 2: Angular momentum quantized: mvr = n h/2π
  • Postulate 3: Radiation only when jump between orbits
  • Radius of nth orbit: rₙ = 0.529 × n²/Z Å (Bohr radius for n=1, Z=1)
  • Velocity: vₙ = 2.18 × 10⁶ Z/n m/s
  • Energy: Eₙ = –13.6 Z²/n² eV (derivation from centripetal = electrostatic)
  • Ionization energy of H = 13.6 eV
  • Energy difference ΔE = 13.6 (1/n₁² – 1/n₂²) eV
  • Successfully explained H-spectrum
  • JEE asks full derivation of Eₙ and rₙ

4. Limitations of Bohr Model

  • Fails for multi-electron atoms (He⁺ ok, but not beyond)
  • Cannot explain fine spectrum (splitting of lines)
  • Cannot explain Zeeman & Stark effect
  • Assumes circular orbits only (elliptical exist)
  • Violates Heisenberg uncertainty principle
  • No concept of wave nature of electron
  • Cannot explain chemical bonding
  • No explanation of relative intensity of lines
  • Replaced by quantum mechanical model
  • Still important for JEE (numericals on H-like atoms)

5. Dual Nature of Matter & de Broglie Relationship

  • de Broglie: All matter has wave nature
  • λ = h / p = h / mv (de Broglie wavelength)
  • For electron, λ = 12.27 / √V Å (V in volt)
  • Davisson-Germer experiment confirmed electron diffraction
  • Macroscopic objects: λ very small → wave nature not observable
  • Microscopic particles (e⁻, p⁺, n): λ comparable → diffraction seen
  • Explained Bohr’s quantization (circumference = nλ)
  • Standing wave concept in orbits
  • JEE asks: Wavelength of 100 eV electron
  • Foundation of wave mechanics

6. Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle

  • Δx · Δp ≥ h/4π (cannot know position & momentum exactly)
  • Δx · Δv ≥ h/4πm
  • ΔE · Δt ≥ h/4π
  • Why? Wave-particle duality
  • Electron cannot exist inside nucleus (Δx small → Δp huge)
  • Explains non-existence of definite orbits
  • Bohr model violates this principle
  • Reason for probabilistic model
  • JEE numericals on minimum uncertainty
  • Fundamental to quantum mechanics

7. Quantum Mechanical Model of Atom

  • Schrödinger wave equation: Ĥψ = Eψ
  • ψ (wave function): Probability amplitude
  • ψ²: Probability density (electron charge cloud)
  • Orbit → Orbital (region of max probability)
  • Three quantum numbers from Schrödinger equation
  • Exact energy only for H-atom & H-like species
  • No definite path, only probability
  • Explains shape, size, energy of orbitals
  • Replaced Bohr’s planetary model
  • Foundation of modern chemistry

8. Quantum Numbers & Shapes of Orbitals

  • Principal quantum number (n): Size & energy (1,2,3...)
  • Azimuthal (l): Shape (0=s, 1=p, 2=d, 3=f); l = 0 to n–1
  • Magnetic (mₗ): Orientation (–l to +l)
  • Spin (mₛ): +½ or –½ (electron spin)
  • Number of orbitals in shell = n²
  • s: Spherical, p: Dumbbell, d: Double dumbbell/clover
  • Radial nodes = n – l – 1
  • Angular nodes = l
  • Total nodes = n – 1
  • ψ vs r: 1s no node, 2s one node, 2p no radial node
  • JEE asks: Nodes in 3p, 4d etc.

9. Rules for Filling Electrons in Orbitals

  • Aufbau principle: Lower (n+l) fills first, same (n+l) → lower n
  • Pauli exclusion: Max 2 electrons per orbital, opposite spin
  • Hund’s rule: Singly occupy degenerate orbitals first, parallel spin
  • Order: 1s < 2s < 2p < 3s < 3p < 4s < 3d < 4p < 5s < 4d < 5p < 6s...
  • Exceptions: Cr = [Ar] 4s¹ 3d⁵, Cu = [Ar] 4s¹ 3d¹⁰
  • Extra stability: Half-filled (p³,d⁵,f⁷) & completely filled (p⁶,d¹⁰,f¹⁴)
  • Due to symmetry & exchange energy
  • Electronic configuration determines chemical properties
  • JEE asks exceptions & magnetic properties
  • Foundation of periodic table
