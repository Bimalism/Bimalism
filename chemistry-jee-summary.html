JEE Chemistry Chapters Summary

Key concepts and formulas for JEE exam preparation

Chapter 1

Some Basic Concepts of Chemistry

  • Mole: n = m/M
  • Avogadro’s number: N_A = 6.022×10²³
  • Molar mass: M = m/n
  • Percentage composition: (mass of element/total mass) × 100
  • Empirical formula: Simplest ratio
  • Molecular formula: n × empirical formula
  • Limiting reagent: Determines product yield
  • Molarity: M = moles of solute/L of solution
  • Molality: m = moles of solute/kg of solvent
  • Mole fraction: x_A = n_A/(n_A + n_B)
Chapter 2

Structure of Atom

  • Bohr’s radius: a_0 = 0.529n²/Z Å
  • Energy of electron: E_n = -13.6Z²/n² eV
  • De Broglie wavelength: λ = h/(mv)
  • Uncertainty principle: Δx·Δp ≥ h/(4π)
  • Quantum numbers: n, l, m_l, m_s
  • Planck’s constant: h = 6.626×10⁻³⁴ Js
  • Aufbau principle: Order of filling orbitals
  • Pauli’s exclusion principle
  • Hund’s rule: Maximum multiplicity
  • Photon energy: E = hν
Chapter 3

Classification of Elements and Periodicity

  • Periodic law: Properties repeat periodically
  • Ionization energy: Energy to remove electron
  • Electronegativity: Pauling scale
  • Atomic radius: Decreases across period
  • Ionic radius: Cations < atoms < anions
  • Electron affinity: Energy change on gaining electron
  • s, p, d, f blocks in periodic table
  • Diagonal relationship: Li-Mg, Be-Al
  • Oxidation states: Trends in groups
  • Metallic character: Increases down group
Chapter 4

Chemical Bonding and Molecular Structure

  • Bond order: ½(N_b - N_a)
  • VSEPR theory: Electron pair repulsion
  • Hybridization: sp, sp², sp³
  • Dipole moment: μ = q × d
  • Fajan’s rule: Polarization of ions
  • Bond energy: Energy to break bond
  • MO theory: Bonding, antibonding orbitals
  • Hydrogen bonding: Intermolecular force
  • Valence bond theory: Orbital overlap
  • Resonance: Delocalized electrons
Chapter 5

States of Matter

  • Ideal gas law: PV = nRT
  • Van der Waals equation: [P + a/V²](V - b) = RT
    • Boyle’s law: PV = constant
  • Charles’ law: V/T = constant
  • Avogadro’s law: V/n = constant
  • Kinetic energy: KE = (3/2)RT
  • Root mean square speed: v_rms = √(3RT/M)
  • Compressibility factor: Z = PV/nRT
  • Critical temperature: T_c = 8a/(27Rb)
  • Mean free path: λ = 1/(√2πd²N)
Chapter 6

Thermodynamics

  • First law: ΔU = q + W
  • Work: W = -PΔV
  • Enthalpy: H = U + PV
  • Entropy: ΔS = q_rev/T
  • Gibbs free energy: ΔG = ΔH - TΔS
  • Hess’s law: ΔH independent of path
  • Standard molar enthalpy: ΔH_f°
  • Bond enthalpy: ΔH = Σ(bond broken) - Σ(bond formed)
  • Second law: ΔS_total ≥ 0
  • Carnot efficiency: η = 1 - (T₂/T₁)
Chapter 7

Equilibrium

  • Equilibrium constant: K_c = [products]/[reactants]
  • Le Chatelier’s principle
  • pH: pH = -log[H⁺]
  • Solubility product: K_sp = [A⁺]^m[B⁻]^n
  • Acid dissociation: K_a = [H⁺][A⁻]/[HA]
  • Base dissociation: K_b = [OH⁻][BH⁺]/[B]
  • Relation: K_w = K_a × K_b = 10⁻¹⁴
  • Buffer: pH = pK_a + log([salt]/[acid])
  • Reaction quotient: Q = [products]/[reactants]
  • Common ion effect
Chapter 8

Redox Reactions

  • Oxidation number: Rules for assignment
  • Redox reaction: Oxidation + Reduction
  • Balancing: Half-reaction method
  • Standard electrode potential: E°
  • Nernst equation: E = E° - (RT/nF)lnQ
  • Faraday’s 1st law: m = ZIt
  • Farwarden’s 2nd law: m₁/m₂ = Z₁/Z₂
  • Electrochemical cell: E_cell = E_cathode - E_anode
  • Galvanic cell: Spontaneous reaction
  • Electrolysis: Non-spontaneous reaction
Chapter 9

Hydrogen

  • Hydrogen isotopes: H-1, H-2, H-3
  • Hydrides: Ionic, covalent, metallic
  • Water hardness: Ca²⁺, Mg²⁺ ions
  • Hydrogen peroxide: H₂O₂, oxidizing agent
  • Ortho and paralue hydrogen
  • Hydrogen bonding: H₂O properties
  • Preparation: Electrolysis, Bosch process
  • Uses: Fuel, ammonia synthesis
  • Heavy water: D₂O, nuclear reactors
  • Hydrogen economy: Fuel cells
Chapter 10

s-Block Elements

  • Group 1: Alkali metals, +1 oxidation
  • Group 2: Alkaline earth metals, +2 oxidation
  • Diagonal relationship: Li-Mg
  • Sodium carbonate: Solvay process
  • Sodium hydroxide: Electrolysis
  • Calcium carbonate: CaCO₃, thermal decomposition
  • Reactivity: Increases down group
  • Flame test: Characteristic colors
  • Hydration energy: Decreases down group
  • Compounds: Oxides, hydroxides, halides
Chapter 11

p-Block Elements

  • Group 13-18: Varied oxidation states
  • Boron: B₂O₃, borax (Na₂B₄O₇·10H₂O)
  • Carbon: Allotropes (diamond, graphite)
  • Nitrogen: NH₃, HNO₃ preparation
  • Oxygen: O₂, O₃ (ozone)
  • Halogens: F₂ to I₂, reactivity decreases
  • Noble gases: Inert, Xe compounds
  • Acidic oxides: CO₂, SO₂
  • Allotropy: P, S forms
  • Oxoacids: HNO₃, H₂SO₄
Chapter 12

Organic Chemistry - Some Basic Principles

  • IUPAC nomenclature: Systematic naming
  • Isomerism: Structural, stereo, geometrical
  • Hybridization: sp³, sp², sp
  • Resonance: Electron delocalization
  • Inductive effect: Electron shift
  • Electromeric effect: Electron shift on reaction
  • Hyperconjugation: Electron delocalization via σ bonds
  • Functional groups: -OH, -CHO, -COOH
  • Reaction intermediates: Carbocation, carbanion
  • Types of reactions: Substitution, elimination
Chapter 13

Hydrocarbons

  • Alkanes: C_nH_{2n+2}
  • Alkenes: C_nH_{2n}
  • Alkynes: C_nH_{2n-2}
  • Aromaticity: Hückel’s rule (4n+2 π electrons)
  • Substitution: Free radical, electrophilic, nucleophilic
  • Addition: Electrophilic, nucleophilic
  • Pyrolysis: Thermal decomposition
  • Ozonolysis: Cleavage of alkenes
  • Markovnikov’s rule: H to less substituted carbon
  • Anti-Markovnikov’s rule: H to more substituted carbon
Chapter 14

Environmental Chemistry

  • Greenhouse effect: CO₂, CH₄
  • Ozone depletion: CFCs, O₃ → O₂
  • Smog: Photochemical, classical
  • Acid rain: SO₂, NOₓ
  • Eutrophication: Nutrient enrichment
  • BOD: Biochemical oxygen demand
  • COD: Chemical oxygen demand
  • Global warming potential: CO₂ = 1, CH₄ = 25
  • Stratospheric ozone: O₃ protection
  • Pollution control: Catalytic converters
Chapter 15

Solutions

  • Raoult’s law: P = x_A P_A + x_B P_B
  • Henry’s law: C = k_H P
  • Molarity: M = moles/L
  • Molality: m = moles/kg
  • Mole fraction: x_A = n_A/(n_A + n_B)
  • Ideal solutions: ΔH_mix = 0
  • Non-ideal solutions: Positive/negative deviation
  • Colligative properties: BP elevation, FP depression
  • Boiling point elevation: ΔT_b = K_b m
  • Freezing point depression: ΔT_f = K_f m
Chapter 16

Electrochemistry

  • Faraday’s 1st law: m = ZIt
  • Faraday’s 2nd law: m₁/m₂ = Z₁/Z₂
  • Nernst equation: E = E° - (RT/nF)lnQ
  • Standard electrode potential: E°
  • Conductivity: κ = 1/ρ
  • Kohlrausch’s law: Λ_m = Λ_m° - K√c
  • Galvanic cell: E_cell = E_cathode - E_anode
  • Electrolytic cell: Non-spontaneous reaction
  • Battery: EMF series
  • Corrosion: Electrochemical process
Chapter 17

Chemical Kinetics

  • Rate law: Rate = k[A]^m[B]^n
  • Order of reaction: m + n
  • Half-life (1st order): t_½ = ln(2)/k
  • Half-life (2nd order): t_½ = 1/(k[A]₀)
  • Arrhenius equation: k = Ae^(-E_a/RT)
  • Rate constant: k
  • Activation energy: E_a
  • First order reaction: [A] = [A]₀e^(-kt)
  • Second order reaction: 1/[A] = kt + 1/[A]₀
  • Collision theory: Reaction rate factors
Chapter 18

Surface Chemistry

  • Adsorption: Surface accumulation
  • Absorption: Bulk penetration
  • Langmuir isotherm: θ = kp/(1 + kp)
  • Catalysis: Lowers E_a
  • Homogeneous catalysis: Same phase
  • Heterogeneous catalysis: Different phases
  • Colloids: 1-1000 nm particles
  • Tyndall effect: Light scattering
  • Emulsions: Liquid-liquid colloids
  • Hardy-Schulz rule: Coagulation
Chapter 19

General Principles and Processes of Isolation

  • Concentration: Gravity separation, froth flotation
  • Roasting: Ore + heat → metal oxide
  • Smelting: Metal oxide + C → metal
  • Electrolytic reduction: Al, Cu
  • Refining: Distillation, liquation
  • Ellingham diagram: ΔG vs T
  • Leaching: Chemical extraction (Au, Ag)
  • Zone refining: Semiconductors
  • Mond’s process: Ni purification
  • Van Arkel process: Zr, Ti purification

  • Coordination compounds: Ligands
  • Magnetic properties: Para-, dia-, ferromagnetic
  • Catalytic properties: d-block
  • Color: d-d transitions
  • Lanthanoid contraction: Size decrease
  • Interstitial compounds: Metal-nonmetal
Chapter 21

Coordination Compounds

  • Coordination number: Number of ligands
  • Ligands: Electron pair donors
  • Valence bond theory: Hybridization
  • Crystal field theory: d-orbital splitting
  • Isomerism: Structural, geometrical, optical
  • Chelate effect: Stable complexes
  • Werner’s theory: Primary, secondary valency
  • Spectrochemical series: Ligand strength
  • Magnetic properties: Spin states
  • Stability constant: K_f = [ML_n]/[M][L]^n
Chapter 22

Haloalkanes and Haloarenes

  • Nucleophilic substitution: S_N1, S_N2
  • Elimination reactions: E1, E2
  • Haloalkanes: R-X (X = halogen)
  • Haloarenes: Ar-X
  • Grignard reagent: R-Mg-X
  • Sandmeyer reaction: Ar-N₂⁺ → Ar-X
  • Fittig reaction: Ar-X + Na → Ar-Ar
  • Wurtz reaction: R-X + Na → R-R
  • Chirality: Optical activity
  • Polyhalogen compounds: CCl₄, DDT
Chapter 23

Alcohols, Phenols and Ethers

  • Alcohols: R-OH, primary, secondary, tertiary
  • Phenols: Ar-OH
  • Ethers: R-O-R
  • Oxidation: Alcohol → aldehyde/ketone
  • Dehydration: Alcohol → alkene
  • Williamson synthesis: Alkoxide + alkyl halide → ether
  • Lucas test: ZnCl₂ + HCl → turbidity
  • Esterification: Alcohol + carboxylic acid → ester
  • Acidic nature: Phenol > alcohol
  • Cleavage: Ethers → alcohols/phenols
Chapter 24

Aldehydes, Ketones and Carboxylic Acids

  • Aldehydes: R-CHO
  • Ketones: R-CO-R
  • Carboxylic acids: R-COOH
  • Cannizzaro reaction: 2R-CHO → R-CH₂OH + R-COO⁻
  • Aldol condensation: Enolate + carbonyl → β-hydroxy carbonyl
  • Oxidation: Aldehyde → carboxylic acid
  • Reduction: Carbonyl → alcohol
  • Acidic strength: Carboxylic acid > phenol
  • Fehling’s test: R-CHO → red precipitate
  • Tollens’ test: R-CHO → silver mirror

  • Enthalpy: H = U + PV

  • Standard entropy change: ΔS°
  • Standard Gibbs free energy: ΔG°
  • Bond energy: Energy to break bond
