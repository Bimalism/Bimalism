1. Photoelectric Effect

  • Ejection of electrons from metal surface by light
  • Occurs only if frequency > threshold frequency (ν₀)
  • Threshold frequency depends on metal (work function)
  • Photoelectrons emitted almost instantaneously (<10⁻⁹ s)
  • Maximum KE of electrons independent of intensity
  • Number of photoelectrons ∝ intensity of light
  • Stopping potential (V₀) stops fastest electrons
  • KE_max = e V₀
  • Failed to be explained by wave theory of light
  • Proved particle nature of light (photons)

2. Hertz and Lenard’s Observations

  • Hertz (1887): UV light helps spark discharge easier
  • UV light ejects electrons from negative plate
  • Lenard (1900): Studied photoelectric effect systematically
  • Observed photocurrent ∝ intensity
  • Found stopping potential independent of intensity
  • Observed threshold frequency phenomenon
  • Electrons emitted only above certain frequency
  • KE increases with frequency, not intensity
  • Instantaneous emission (no time lag)
  • Wave theory could not explain these results

3. Einstein’s Photoelectric Equation & Particle Nature

  • Light consists of photons: E = hν
  • Einstein (1905): One photon ejects one electron
  • hν = φ + KE_max (φ = work function)
  • KE_max = hν – hν₀ = h(ν – ν₀)
  • Stopping potential: eV₀ = h(ν – ν₀)
  • V₀ vs ν graph is straight line with slope h/e
  • Intercept on frequency axis = ν₀
  • Explained all observations of photoelectric effect
  • Proved light has particle nature (photon)
  • Einstein got Nobel Prize in 1921 for this

4. Matter Waves & de Broglie Relation

  • de Broglie (1924): Matter has wave nature
  • Wavelength λ = h / p = h / (m v)
  • h = Planck’s constant = 6.626 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s
  • Duality: All particles have both wave & particle nature
  • Macroscopic objects: λ very small → wave nature not seen
  • Electrons, protons: λ significant → wave nature observable
  • Faster particle → smaller wavelength
  • Explained Bohr’s quantization (standing waves)
  • Foundation of wave mechanics/quantum mechanics
  • λ = h / √(2 m E) for non-relativistic particles

5. Davisson-Germer Experiment (1927)

  • Confirmed wave nature of electrons
  • Electrons accelerated through V volts → λ = h/√(2meV)
  • Scattered from nickel crystal (natural diffraction grating)
  • Observed diffraction pattern with maxima
  • Maxima at same angle as X-ray diffraction
  • Measured wavelength matched de Broglie formula
  • Experimental wavelength = 0.165 nm (theory = 0.167 nm)
  • Proved electrons behave as waves
  • Direct evidence of de Broglie hypothesis
  • Established wave-particle duality for matter
