Example: Multi-phase 1D Motion

A particle starts from rest with acceleration 2 m/s² for 5s, then moves with constant velocity for 10s, then decelerates at 4 m/s² until it stops. Find total distance covered.

Solution: Phase 1: s₁ = 0 + ½×2×25 = 25m, v = 0+2×5 = 10 m/s

Phase 2: s₂ = 10×10 = 100m

Phase 3: 0² = 10² + 2×(-4)×s₃ → s₃ = 100/8 = 12.5m

Total distance = 25 + 100 + 12.5 = 137.5 m

3. Equations of Motion & Gravity Effects

Constant Acceleration Equations

① v = u + at (Velocity as function of time)
② s = ut + ½ at² (Displacement as function of time)
③ v² = u² + 2as (Velocity as function of displacement)
④ sₙ = u + ½a(2n-1) (Displacement during nth second)
⑤ v̄ = (u + v)/2 = s/t (Average velocity relation)

Derivation of Third Equation of Motion

Starting from v = u + at → t = (v-u)/a

Substitute into s = ut + ½at²:

s = u[(v-u)/a] + ½a[(v-u)/a]²

s = (uv - u²)/a + (v² - 2uv + u²)/(2a)

Multiply through by 2a: 2as = 2uv - 2u² + v² - 2uv + u²

Simplify: 2as = v² - u² ∴ v² = u² + 2as

Example: Optimal River Crossing

A river flows east at 4 m/s. A swimmer can swim at 5 m/s in still water. How should they swim to cross 60m wide river in minimum time? What is this minimum time?

Solution: For minimum time, swim perpendicular to banks: directly north

Time = width / north component = 60 / 5 = 12 seconds

Drift downstream = river velocity × time = 4 × 12 = 48m east

Actual path is diagonal with resultant velocity = √(5² + 4²) = √41 ≈ 6.4 m/s

5. Two-Dimensional & Projectile Motion

Projectile Motion Fundamental Equations

Horizontal component: x = (u cosθ) t, vₓ = u cosθ (constant)
Vertical component: y = (u sinθ) t - ½ gt², vᵧ = u sinθ - gt
Resultant velocity: v = √(vₓ² + vᵧ²)
Velocity direction: tanφ = vᵧ/vₓ (φ measured from horizontal)
Path equation: y = x tanθ - [gx²/(2u² cos²θ)] (parabolic trajectory)

Example: Projectile from Elevated Position

A ball is thrown from 25m high building at 15 m/s, 37° above horizontal (sin37°=0.6, cos37°=0.8). Find impact velocity and distance from building.

Solution: Vertical motion: -25 = (15×0.6)t - 5t² → 5t² - 9t - 25 = 0

t = [9 ± √(81+500)]/10 = [9 ± √581]/10 ≈ [9 ± 24.1]/10 → t ≈ 3.31s (positive)

Horizontal range: R = 15×0.8×3.31 = 39.72m

Impact vₓ = 12 m/s, vᵧ = 9 - 10×3.31 = -24.1 m/s

Impact velocity = √(12² + 24.1²) = √(144+580.8) = √724.8 ≈ 26.92 m/s

Example: Vertical Circle Energy Analysis

A 0.5kg mass swings in vertical circle of radius 2m. Find minimum speed at bottom to complete circle, and tension at bottom if speed is 1.2 times minimum.

Solution: Minimum speed at bottom: v_min = √(5rg) = √(5×10×2) = √100 = 10 m/s

8. Advanced Topics & Applications

Variable Acceleration Methods

When acceleration depends on time a = f(t): v = u + ∫₀ᵗ a dt, s = ut + ∫₀ᵗ [∫₀ᵗ a dt] dt
When acceleration depends on velocity a = f(v): t = ∫ᵤᵛ dv/a(v), or use a = v dv/ds for position relation
When acceleration depends on position a = f(s): ∫ v dv = ∫ a ds (energy approach often useful)

Example: Velocity-Dependent Acceleration

A particle moves with a = -k√v (deceleration proportional to square root of velocity). If initial velocity is v₀, find time to stop and stopping distance.

Solution: dv/dt = -k√v → dv/√v = -k dt

∫ dv/√v = -k∫dt → 2√v = -kt + 2√v₀

When v=0: 0 = -kt + 2√v₀ → t_stop = 2√v₀/k

For distance: v dv/ds = -k√v → √v dv = -k ds

∫ √v dv = -k∫ds → (2/3)v^(3/2) = -ks + (2/3)v₀^(3/2)

When v=0: s_stop = (2v₀^(3/2))/(3k)

Resistive Medium Dynamics

Low-speed drag: F_d ∝ v (Stokes' law regime)
High-speed drag: F_d ∝ v² (Newtonian regime)
Terminal velocity occurs when drag force equals net driving force

Air resistance reduces maximum achievable height

Example: Pendulum in Decelerating Car

A simple pendulum hangs in car decelerating at 3 m/s². Find new equilibrium angle and period if L=1m.

Solution: Effective g: g_eff = √(g² + a²) = √(10² + 3²) = √109 ≈ 10.44 m/s²

Equilibrium angle from vertical: tanθ = a/g = 3/10 = 0.3 → θ ≈ 16.7°

Period: T = 2π√(L/g_eff) = 2π√(1/10.44) ≈ 2π√0.0958 ≈ 1.94s (compared to 2.01s at rest)

  1. Identify all known quantities and target unknowns
  2. Select appropriate reference frame (inertial or non-inertial)

  5. Check dimensional consistency of intermediate steps

  8. Utilize symmetry properties to reduce computational effort
