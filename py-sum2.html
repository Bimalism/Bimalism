3. Equations of Motion (Constant a)

① v = u + at
② s = ut + ½ at²
③ v² = u² + 2as
④ sₙₜₕ = u + a/2 (2n−1)
⑤ Average velocity = (u + v)/2

Free Fall & Motion under Gravity

Take downward direction positive:
u = 0 (if dropped), a = +g = 9.8 m/s²
Upward throw: a = −g

Time of flight (thrown up): T = 2u/g
Max height: H = u²/2g

4. Relative Velocity

v⃗_AB = v⃗_A − v⃗_B

5. Motion in a Plane – Projectile Motion

Horizontal: x = (u cosθ) t
Vertical: y = (u sinθ) t − ½ gt²
vₓ = u cosθ (constant)
vᵧ = u sinθ − gt

Key Formulae (θ = angle of projection)

Time of flight T2u sinθ / g
Maximum height Hu² sin²θ / 2g
Range Ru² sin2θ / g
Max Range (θ)45°
Equation of trajectoryy = x tanθ − gx²/(2u² cos²θ)

Symmetric Nature

Time to reach max height = T/2
Velocity at same height is same in magnitude on upward & downward journey

6. Uniform Circular Motion

Angular velocity ωv/r = 2π/T = 2πf
Centripetal acc.a_c = v²/r = ω²r (towards centre)
Period T2πr/v
No tangential acceleration in UCM

Conical Pendulum, Banked Road, etc. use same a_c formula

Quick Revision Formula Sheet

All Important Formulae at One Place

1D (const a): v=u+at, s=ut+½at², v²=u²+2as

Projectile: R = u² sin2θ/g, H = u² sin²θ/2g, T = 2u sinθ/g

UCM: a_c = v²/r = ω²r, T = 2πr/v

Relative: v_AB = v_A − v_B

Max height velocity = 0 (only vertical component)

Range same for θ and (90°−θ)
