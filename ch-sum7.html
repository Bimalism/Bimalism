1. Oxidation Number Rules

  • Pure element → O.N. = 0
  • Monoatomic ion → O.N. = charge
  • F always –1, O usually –2 (except peroxides –1, OF₂ +2)
  • H +1 (except metal hydrides –1)
  • Alkali metals +1, Alkaline earth +2
  • Sum of O.N. in compound = 0, in ion = charge
  • Maximum O.N. = group number, Minimum = group number – 8
  • In KO₂ → K⁺, O = –½ (superoxide)
  • Cr in Cr₂O₇²⁻ → +6, in CrO₅ → +6 (with two peroxide links)
  • JEE loves: Find O.N. of S in S₈, SO₂, H₂SO₅, Na₂S₄O₆

2. Balancing Redox Reactions

  • Oxidation number method: Balance increase/decrease in O.N.
  • Half-reaction method: Split into oxidation & reduction halves
  • Acidic medium: Use H⁺ and H₂O
  • Basic medium: Add OH⁻ on both sides, form H₂O
  • MnO₄⁻ → Mn²⁺ (acidic): +5e⁻, 8H⁺ → 4H₂O
  • Cr₂O₇²⁻ → Cr³⁺: +6e⁻, 14H⁺ → 7H₂O
  • Balancing in basic: First balance as acidic, then add OH⁻
  • Disproportionation: Same species oxidized & reduced (H₂O₂, Cl₂)
  • JEE favourite: Balance MnO₄⁻ + Fe²⁺, I⁻ + IO₃⁻
  • Never forget to balance charge in half reactions

3. Types of Redox Reactions

  • Combination: A + B → C (e.g., 2Mg + O₂ → 2MgO)
  • Decomposition: C → A + B (thermal/photochemical)
  • Displacement: A + BC → AC + B (metal + acid)
  • Disproportionation: Same element → two different O.N.
  • Comproportionation: Two O.N. → intermediate (reverse)
  • Examples: Cl₂ + H₂O → HCl + HOCl (disprop)
  • 2Cu⁺ → Cu + Cu²⁺ (disprop), Cu + Cu²⁺ → 2Cu⁺ (comprop)
  • Intramolecular redox: NH₄NO₂ → N₂ + 2H₂O
  • JEE asks: Identify type of redox reaction
  • All combustion reactions are redox

4. Conductance in Electrolytic Solutions

  • Conductance (G) = 1/R, Siemens (S)
  • Specific conductance (κ) = G × (l/A)
  • Molar conductance (Λ_m) = κ × 1000 / C
  • Λ_m ↑ with dilution for all electrolytes
  • Strong electrolytes: Slight ↑ due to less ion pairing
  • Weak electrolytes: Sharp ↑ due to more ionization
  • Λ_m vs √C plot: Straight line for strong (Debye-Hückel-Onsager)
  • Unit of κ → S cm⁻¹, Λ_m → S cm² mol⁻¹
  • Cell constant = l/A, measured using 0.1 N KCl
  • JEE loves: Calculate Λ_m from κ and concentration

5. Kohlrausch’s Law

  • Λ°_m (electrolyte) = ν₊ λ°₊ + ν₋ λ°₋
  • Limiting molar conductance at infinite dilution
  • Additivity of ionic conductances
  • λ°(H⁺) = 349.8, λ°(OH⁻) = 199.1 → highest mobility
  • Application: Find Λ°_m of weak electrolytes (CH₃COOH)
  • Λ°(CH₃COOH) = Λ°(CH₃COONa) + Λ°(HCl) – Λ°(NaCl)
  • Degree of dissociation α = Λ_m / Λ°_m
  • K_a = α²C / (1–α) for weak acid
  • JEE favourite: Calculate K_a of HF using Kohlrausch
  • Transport number: λ₊ / (λ₊ + λ₋)

6. Electrochemical Cells

  • Galvanic cell: Spontaneous, chemical → electrical
  • Electrolytic cell: Non-spontaneous, electrical → chemical
  • Anode: Oxidation, Cathode: Reduction
  • SHE: H₂(1 atm) | H⁺(1 M) || E° = 0.00 V
  • E°(Zn²⁺/Zn) = –0.76 V, E°(Cu²⁺/Cu) = +0.34 V
  • Daniell cell: Zn | Zn²⁺ || Cu²⁺ | Cu → E_cell = 1.10 V
  • Salt bridge: Maintains neutrality (KCl, KNO₃)
  • EMF = E_cathode – E_anode (including sign)
  • Galvanic cell: E_cell > 0, ΔG < 0
  • JEE asks: Which is anode in Cu-Zn cell?

7. Nernst Equation & Applications

  • E = E° – (RT/nF) ln Q = E° – (0.0591/n) log Q at 298 K
  • E_cell = E°_cell – (0.0591/n) log (1/[reactants])
  • At equilibrium: E_cell = 0 → E°_cell = (0.0591/n) log K
  • ΔG = –nFE_cell, ΔG° = –nFE°_cell
  • Concentration cells: E_cell = (0.0591/n) log (C₂/C₁)
  • pH measurement using glass electrode
  • Solubility product from cell potential
  • Potentiometric titration: Fe²⁺ vs KMnO₄, Ag⁺ vs Cl⁻
  • JEE favourite: Calculate E_cell for non-standard conditions
  • log K = n E°_cell / 0.0591
E_cell = E°_cell – (0.059/n) log Q

8. Batteries & Fuel Cells

  • Primary cell: Dry cell (Zn | NH₄Cl | MnO₂), 1.5 V
  • Secondary cell: Lead accumulator (Pb | PbSO₄ | H₂SO₄)
  • Charging: PbSO₄ → Pb + PbO₂
  • Li-ion battery: Highest energy density
  • Fuel cell: H₂-O₂ → H₂O, E = 1.23 V, 70% efficient
  • No recharging needed, continuous fuel supply
  • Mercury cell: Constant voltage 1.35 V
  • Ni-Cd cell: Rechargeable, memory effect
  • Corrosion: Electrochemical rusting of Fe
  • JEE asks: Why fuel cell is future of energy?

9. JEE Must-Revise Points (Daily)

  • E_cell = E_cathode – E_anode → always positive for galvanic
  • Nernst: E = E° – (0.059/n) log Q
  • log K = n E° / 0.0591
  • Kohlrausch: Λ° = sum of ionic conductances
  • Oxidation number of O in peroxide = –1
  • MnO₄⁻ in acidic medium gains 5e⁻
  • H⁺ and OH⁻ have highest λ°
  • ΔG = –nFE_cell
  • Anode: negative in galvanic, positive in electrolytic
  • Fuel cell: Highest efficiency, no pollution
