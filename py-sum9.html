1. Periodic Motion • Frequency • Time Period

Periodic Motion: Motion that repeats after a fixed time interval
Time Period (T): Time for one complete oscillation → Unit: s
Frequency (f): f = 1/T → Unit: Hz
Angular frequency (ω): ω = 2πf = 2π/T
General equation: x(t) = A cos(ωt + φ) or A sin(ωt + φ)
Velocity: v = −A ω sin(ωt + φ)
Acceleration: a = −A ω² cos(ωt + φ) = −ω² x

2. Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM)

Defining Equation of SHM

d²x/dt² + ω² x = 0  →  a ∝ −x (restoring)

Solution: x = A cos(ωt + φ)
Maximum displacement (Amplitude): A
Maximum velocity: v_max = A ω
Maximum acceleration: a_max = A ω²

3. Energy in Simple Harmonic Motion

Kinetic Energy: KE = (1/2) m ω² (A² − x²)
Potential Energy: PE = (1/2) m ω² x²
Total Energy: E = (1/2) m ω² A² = constant

Key Points

• At mean position (x=0): KE max, PE = 0
• At extreme (x=±A): KE = 0, PE max
• KE + PE = constant

5. Wave Motion • Types • Speed

Wave equation: y = A sin(kx − ωt + φ)
Wave number: k = 2π/λ
Wave speed: v = f λ = ω/k
Speed on string: v = √(T/μ)  (T = tension, μ = mass/length)

6. Superposition • Reflection • Doppler Effect

Principle of Superposition
When two or more waves meet, resultant displacement = vector sum of individual displacements
Reflection:
Fixed end → π phase change
Free end → No phase change
Doppler Effect (sound):
f' = f (v ± v₀)/(v ± vₛ)
• v₀ = observer speed (towards +)
• vₛ = source speed (towards +)

Common Cases

Source moving towards stationary observer: f' = f (v/(v − vₛ))
Observer moving towards stationary source: f' = f ((v + v₀)/v)
