1. Sexual Reproduction in Flowering Plants

  • Flower is modified shoot for sexual reproduction; typical flower has calyx, corolla, androecium, gynoecium.
  • Microsporangium → pollen grain (male gametophyte): vegetative cell + 2 male gametes; develops by microsporogenesis.
  • Megasporangium (ovule) → embryo sac (female gametophyte): 7-celled, 8-nucleate (egg apparatus, 3 antipodals, 2 polar nuclei).
  • Pollination: self (autogamy, geitonogamy) and cross (xenogamy); agents – wind (anemophily), water (hydrophily), insects (entomophily).
  • Outbreeding devices: dichogamy, protandry/protogyny, self-incompatibility, dicliny, herkogamy.
  • Pollen-pistil interaction: pollen lands → recognition → acceptance/rejection → pollen tube growth.
  • Double fertilization: one male gamete + egg → zygote (2n); second male gamete + 2 polar nuclei → PEN (3n) → endosperm.
  • Endosperm development (mostly nuclear type) precedes embryo; embryo: embryogeny → globular → heart → mature embryo.
  • Seed = ovule after fertilization (testa, tegmen, embryo, endosperm/cotyledons); fruit = matured ovary (pericarp).
  • Special modes: Apomixis (asexual seed), Parthenocarpy (seedless fruit), Polyembryony (multiple embryos in one seed).

2. Human Reproduction

  • Male reproductive system: testes (seminiferous tubules + Leydig cells), epididymis, vas deferens, accessory glands, penis.
  • Female reproductive system: ovaries, fallopian tubes, uterus, cervix, vagina, external genitalia.
  • Testis: spermatogonium → primary spermatocyte → meiosis I → secondary spermatocyte → meiosis II → spermatids → spermatozoa (64 days).
  • Ovary: oogonium → primary oocyte (arrested at prophase I till puberty) → meiosis I → secondary oocyte + 1st polar body → ovulation.
  • Menstrual cycle (28 days): menstrual → proliferative (FSH, estrogen) → ovulation (LH surge, day 14) → secretory (progesterone).
  • Fertilization in ampullary-isthmic junction → zygote → morula → blastocyst → implantation (day 6–7).
  • Blastocyst: inner cell mass → embryo; trophoblast → placenta (hCG, hPL, relaxin).
  • Pregnancy: embryonic period (0–8 weeks, organogenesis), foetal period (9 weeks–birth); placenta provides nutrition, O₂, removes waste.
  • Parturition: initiated by foetal ejection reflex → oxytocin → uterine contractions; fully developed foetus at 9 months.
  • Lactation: prolactin (milk production), oxytocin (milk ejection); colostrum first milk rich in IgA.

3. Reproductive Health

  • Reproductive health = complete physical, emotional, behavioural and social well-being in reproduction.
  • STDs: gonorrhoea, syphilis, chlamydia, trichomoniasis, hepatitis-B, HIV; prevented by condoms & awareness.
  • Contraceptive methods: Natural (periodic abstinence, withdrawal), Barrier (condom, diaphragm), IUDs (Cu-T, LNG-20), Oral pills, Implants, Injectables, Surgical (vasectomy, tubectomy).
  • Medical Termination of Pregnancy (MTP) legal up to 20 weeks in India.
  • Amniocentesis: sampling amniotic fluid to detect chromosomal abnormalities (e.g., Down syndrome) and sex-linked disorders (now banned for sex determination).
  • Infertility causes: congenital, physical, immunological, psychological.
  • ART: IVF (test tube baby), ZIFT (zygote intra-fallopian transfer), GIFT (gamete intra-fallopian transfer), IUI, ICSI.
  • IVF: oocytes + sperm cultured → embryo (8-cell stage) transferred to fallopian tube (ZIFT) or uterus (IUT).
  • First test tube baby (India): Harsha (1986) by Dr. Indira Hinduja.
  • Population explosion controlled by raising marriageable age, education, family planning programmes (RCH).
