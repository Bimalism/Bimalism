1. Classification & IUPAC Nomenclature

  • Haloalkanes: Halogen attached to sp³ carbon (alkyl halide)
  • Haloarenes: Halogen attached directly to benzene ring
  • Classification: 1°, 2°, 3° (based on carbon bearing X)
  • Vinylic halide: X on C of C=C
  • Benzylic halide: X on carbon adjacent to benzene
  • IUPAC: Halogen as prefix (fluoro, chloro, bromo, iodo)
  • Order of naming: Functional group > multiple bond > halo
  • Gem-dihalide: Two X on same carbon
  • Vicinal dihalide: X on adjacent carbons
  • JEE loves: Correct IUPAC name of complex halo compounds

2. Nature of C–X Bond & Physical Properties

  • C–X bond is polar (Cδ+ – Xδ–) due to electronegativity difference
  • Bond length: C–F < C–Cl < C–Br < C–I
  • Bond strength: C–F > C–Cl > C–Br > C–I
  • Boiling point order: RI > RBr > RCl > RF (van der Waals forces)
  • Density order: RI > RBr > RCl > RF
  • Alkyl iodides are purple in color with I₂
  • Dipole moment: CH₃Cl > CH₃F (bond length factor)
  • Chloroform is stored in dark bottles with ethanol (prevents phosgene)
  • Haloarenes less polar than haloalkanes (sp² C–X bond)
  • JEE asks: Why C–F bond strongest but RF has lowest BP?

3. General Methods of Preparation

  • From alcohols: ROH + HX (ZnCl₂ for 1°), PCl₅, PCl₃, SOCl₂ (best)
  • Halogen exchange: Finkelstein (RCl/RBr → RI with NaI/acetone)
  • Hunsdiecker: RCOOA→ RBr + CO₂ (with Br₂)
  • From alkenes: HX (Markovnikov), HBr + peroxide (anti-M)
  • From alkynes: HX (two times → gem-dihalide)
  • Swarts reaction: RCl → RF with Hg₂F₂ or SbF₃
  • Borodine-Hunsdiecker for bromides
  • Direct halogenation of hydrocarbons (free radical)
  • Sandmeyer/Gatterman for haloarenes from diazonium
  • JEE favourite: Best method for RI from ROH

4. Nucleophilic Substitution Reactions (SN1 & SN2)

  • SN2: Bimolecular, one-step, inversion, 1° best, strong Nu
  • SN1: Unimolecular, two-step, carbocation, racemisation, 3° best
  • Order of reactivity: RI > RBr > RCl > RF
  • SN2 rate depends on [RX] and [Nu⁻]
  • SN1 rate depends only on [RX]
  • Solvent: Polar protic → favours SN1, Polar aprotic → favours SN2
  • Allylic & benzylic halides → highly reactive (resonance stabilized carbocation)
  • Strong base/Nu → favours SN2/E2
  • Ambident nucleophiles: CN⁻ (C or N), NO₂⁻
  • JEE loves: Predict mechanism & product for given substrate

5. Elimination Reactions (E1 & E2)

  • E2: One-step, anti-periplanar, strong base, 3° best
  • E1: Two-step, carbocation intermediate, 3° best
  • Zaitsev’s rule: Major product = more substituted alkene
  • Hofmann product: Bulky base → less substituted alkene
  • Dehydrohalogenation: Alcoholic KOH
  • E2 favoured by strong base, high temp, poor leaving group
  • Order of reactivity in elimination: RI > RBr > RCl > RF
  • Competition: SN1/E1 or SN2/E2 depending on conditions
  • High temperature favours elimination
  • JEE asks: Major product with alc. KOH vs aq. KOH

6. Haloarenes – Reactions & Properties

  • C–X bond in haloarene has partial double bond character (resonance)
  • Less reactive than haloalkanes toward nucleophilic substitution
  • Reimer-Tiemann: Phenol + CHCl₃ + KOH → salicylaldehyde
  • Wurtz-Fittig: ArX + RX + Na → Ar–R
  • Fittig: 2 ArX + Na → Ar–Ar
  • Electrophilic aromatic substitution (EAS) possible (halogen is o/p director but deactivating)
  • Rasching process: Chlorobenzene from benzene + HCl + air
  • Dow’s process: Chlorobenzene from phenol
  • High boiling point and insoluble in water
  • JEE loves: Why haloarene does not undergo SN reaction easily

7. Polyhalogen Compounds & Environmental Effects

  • Chloroform: CHCl₃, anaesthetic, forms phosgene (COCl₂ + HCl)
  • Iodoform: CHI₃, antiseptic, yellow ppt with CH₃COR or CH₃CH(OH)R
  • Carbon tetrachloride: CCl₄, fire extinguisher (phosgene issue)
  • Freons (CFCs): Cause ozone depletion (Cl• radical chain)
  • DDT: Persistent pesticide, bioaccumulation, banned in many countries
  • Trihalomethanes in water → carcinogenic
  • Westron & Westrosol: Acetylene tetrachloride & pentachloroethane
  • Teflon: Polymer of tetrafluoroethene
  • Phosgene: Highly toxic, used in warfare
  • JEE asks: Environmental hazard of CFCs & DDT

8. JEE Must-Revise Points (Daily)

  • Reactivity order: RI > RBr > RCl > RF (both SN & E)
  • SN1 → 3° > 2° > 1°, racemisation, polar protic solvent
  • SN2 → 1° > 2° > 3°, inversion, polar aprotic solvent
  • Alc. KOH → elimination, Aq. KOH → substitution
  • Chloroform + air + light → phosgene (toxic)
  • CFCs destroy ozone layer; DDT is non-biodegradable
  • Haloarene inert due to resonance & sp² C–X bond
  • Iodoform test positive for methyl ketones & acetaldehyde
  • Best reagent for ROH → RCl: SOCl₂ (gaseous byproducts)
  • Ambident nucleophile: SCN⁻ gives R–SCN (SN2) & R–NCS (SN1)
