1. General Introduction & Trends

  • P-block: Elements with valence electrons in p-orbitals (ns²np¹⁻⁶)
  • Electronic configuration: ns²np¹ to ns²np⁶ (Group 13 to 18)
  • First element anomaly: Small size, high IE, high electronegativity, non-metal nature
  • Down the group: Atomic size ↑, IE ↓, metallic character ↑, reducing nature ↑
  • Across period: Non-metallic → metallic character decreases then increases
  • Inert pair effect: Stability of +1/+3 > +3/+5 and +2/+4 > +4/+6 down the group
  • Maximum covalency: Limited by availability of d-orbitals (except first member)
  • Oxidation states: Group number – 10 (for ns²np¹⁻⁶)
  • Diagonal relationship seen with s-block elements
  • JEE loves: Inert pair effect, anomalous behaviour of first element

2. Group 13 – Boron Family

  • Boron is non-metal, others are metals
  • Boron forms covalent compounds, Al shows some ionic character
  • Boron: Electron deficient, forms B₂H₆ (3c-2e bonds), no cation
  • Al forms Al³⁺, amphoteric oxide Al₂O₃, reacts with alkali (Al + NaOH → NaAlO₂)
  • Inert pair effect: Tl⁺ more stable than Tl³⁺
  • Borax bead test: Gives coloured beads with transition metals
  • Boric acid H₃BO₃: Monobasic, Lewis acid, accepts OH⁻
  • Al₂Cl₆ dimer (3c-4e bonds), BCl₃ monomer
  • Al is most abundant metal in earth’s crust
  • JEE favourite: Reaction of Al with NaOH, anomalous properties of boron

3. Group 14 – Carbon Family

  • Catenation: C >> Si > Ge > Sn > Pb
  • Allotropy: C (diamond, graphite), Sn (grey/white), Pb only metallic
  • CO₂ acidic, SiO₂ weakly acidic, SnO₂/PbO₂ amphoteric
  • Inert pair effect: +2 state more stable in Sn and Pb
  • CO strong reducing, SiO strong reducing agent at high T
  • Sn + conc. HNO₃ → β-stannic acid (insoluble)
  • Pb + conc. HNO₃ → Pb(NO₃)₂ (soluble)
  • Carbon forms multiple bonds easily (pπ–pπ), others form less
  • CO has triple bond, strongest M–C bond
  • JEE loves: Heating of Sn/Pb with conc. HNO₃, catenation trend

4. Group 15 – Nitrogen Family

  • N₂ triple bond, very stable, P–P single bonds in P₄
  • N shows +1 to +5, Bi mainly +3 (inert pair)
  • NH₃ basic, PH₃ weakly basic, AsH₃/SbH₃/BiH₃ neutral
  • HNO₃ strong oxidising, P forms oxyacids (H₃PO₃ dibasic)
  • P₄ + hot conc. NaOH → PH₃ + NaH₂PO₂ (disproportionation)
  • N₂ → NH₃ (Haber), N₂ → NO (Birkeland-Eyde)
  • PCl₅ gaseous monomeric, solid ionic [PCl₄]⁺[PCl₆]⁻
  • Bi₂O₅ least stable oxide, N₂O₅ most acidic
  • Thermal stability: NH₃ > PH₃ > AsH₃ > SbH₃ > BiH₃
  • JEE favourite: Disproportionation of P in alkali, PCl₅ structure

5. Group 16 – Oxygen Family

  • O₂ paramagnetic, S₈ ring, Se/Te chain, Po metallic
  • Acidic nature: SO₂ > SeO₂ > TeO₂ > PoO₂
  • H₂O neutral, H₂S weakly acidic, H₂Te more acidic
  • SO₂ reducing (except with strong oxidants), SO₃ oxidising
  • O₃ powerful oxidising agent, used in ozonolysis
  • SF₆ inert, octahedral, no hydrolysis
  • H₂SO₄ dehydrating agent, oxidising in conc. form
  • Volatility: H₂O > H₂Te > H₂Se > H₂S (due to H-bonding)
  • Thermal stability: H₂O > H₂S > H₂Se > H₂Te
  • JEE loves: Oxidising action of O₃, SF₆ inertness, SO₂ bleaching

6. Group 17 – Halogens

  • F₂ most reactive halogen, pale yellow gas
  • Bond energy: F–F weak (156 kJ/mol) due to lone pair repulsion
  • Oxidising power: F₂ > Cl₂ > Br₂ > I₂
  • HF weakest acid (strong H-bonding), HI strongest
  • Interhalogen compounds: XX’₃ T-shaped, XX’₅ square pyramidal
  • Cl₂ + hot conc. NaOH → NaClO₃ (chlorate), cold dilute → NaClO
  • I₂ + starch → blue-black complex
  • AgF soluble, AgCl/AgBr/AgI insoluble
  • F₂ reacts with water violently → O₂ + O₃
  • JEE favourite: Reaction of Cl₂ with NaOH (hot/cold), HF acid strength

7. Group 18 – Noble Gases

  • Full octet, very stable, low boiling points
  • Xe forms XeF₂ (linear), XeF₄ (sq. planar), XeF₆ (distorted octahedral)
  • Xe + F₂ (1:20, 673K) → XeF₆, Xe + O₂F₂ → XeO₃F₂
  • XeF₂, XeF₄, XeF₆ strong fluorinating agents
  • XeO₃ pyramidal, explosive; XeO₄ tetrahedral
  • XeF₆ + H₂O → XeOF₄ + 2HF (partial hydrolysis)
  • KrF₂ known, linear, less stable than Xe compounds
  • Helium best refrigerant (lowest b.p.)
  • Only Xe forms stable compounds (high IE of He, Ne)
  • JEE loves: Structure of XeF₂/XeF₄/XeF₆, hydrolysis reactions

8. JEE Must-Revise Points (Daily Revision)

  • Inert pair effect strongest in Group 13 & 14 lower elements
  • First element always non-metal, covalent, anomalous
  • F₂ > Cl₂ > Br₂ > I₂ in oxidising power, reverse in acid strength of HX
  • Only Xe forms maximum compounds among noble gases
  • SF₆ inert, no hydrolysis; PCl₅ hydrolyses completely
  • Al₂O₃ amphoteric, CO₂ acidic, SiO₂ weakly acidic
  • PCl₅ solid ionic [PCl₄]⁺[PCl₆]⁻
  • Hot conc. Cl₂ + NaOH → chlorate, cold dilute → hypochlorite
  • XeF₆ distorted octahedral due to lone pair
  • Disproportionation: P₄ + NaOH → PH₃ + H₂PO₂⁻
