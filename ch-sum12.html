1. Introduction & Werner's Theory

  • Coordination compounds: Central metal atom/ion + ligands
  • Coordination entity: [M(L)n]ⁿ⁺/ⁿ⁻ (in bracket)
  • Werner: Distinguished primary (ionizable) & secondary (non-ionizable) valency
  • Coordination number = secondary valency
  • Oxidation state = primary valency
  • Example: CoCl₃·6NH₃ → [Co(NH₃)₆]Cl₃ (CN = 6)
  • Complex ion may be cation, anion or neutral
  • Charge on complex = charge on metal + total charge on ligands
  • Werner explained isomerism in coordination compounds
  • JEE loves: Number of ions from conductivity & Werner postulates

2. Ligands, Denticity & Chelation

  • Ligand: Electron pair donor (Lewis base)
  • Denticity: No. of donor atoms in one ligand (mono=1, bidentate=2, etc.)
  • Ambidentate: Can bind from two sites (NO₂⁻ → M–NO₂ or M–ONO)
  • Chelate: Bidentate or polydentate ligand forming ring (stable)
  • Chelate effect: Chelated complexes more stable than similar non-chelated
  • Common bidentate: en, ox²⁻, acac⁻
  • Flexidentate: Can change denticity (EDTA usually hexadentate)
  • Coordination number usually 4 or 6 (tetrahedral or octahedral)
  • CN = 2 (linear), 3 (rare), 8 (dodecahedral), 12 (fcc)
  • JEE favourite: Identify denticity & ambidentate ligands

3. IUPAC Nomenclature

  • Cation named first, then anion
  • Ligands in alphabetical order (ignore prefixes)
  • Anionic ligand ends with ‘o’ (chloro, cyano), neutral have name as is (aqua, ammine)
  • Prefix: di, tri, tetra…; bis, tris for complex ligands
  • Oxidation state of metal in Roman numeral in parenthesis
  • For anionic complex: metal name ends with ‘ate’
  • Geometry prefix: fac-, mer-, cis-, trans-
  • Example: [Co(NH₃)₄Cl₂]Cl → tetraamminedichloridocobalt(III) chloride
  • [Cr(H₂O)₄Cl₂]Cl → tetraaquadichloridochromium(III) chloride
  • JEE loves: Write correct IUPAC name

4. Isomerism in Coordination Compounds

  • Structural isomerism: Ionisation, linkage, coordination, solvate, ligand
  • Stereoisomerism: Geometrical (cis/trans) & optical
  • Geometrical in square planar: [Ma₂b₂], [Mabcd] (cis/trans)
  • Geometrical in octahedral: [Ma₄b₂] (cis/trans), [Ma₃b₃] (fac/mer)
  • Optical isomerism: Requires chirality (no plane of symmetry)
  • [M(AA)₃], [M(AB)₃], cis-[M(AA)₂a₂] show optical isomerism
  • Trans isomers never optically active
  • Linkage: SCN⁻ (thiocyanato-S or isothiocyanato-N)
  • Coordination isomerism: When cation & anion both complex
  • JEE favourite: Number of isomers of [Ma₄b₂], [M(AA)₃]

5. Valence Bond Theory (VBT)

  • Metal uses hybrid orbitals to accept lone pairs
  • Octahedral → d²sp³ (inner) or sp³d² (outer)
  • Inner orbital (low spin): Uses (n–1)d orbitals → d²sp³
  • Outer orbital (high spin): Uses nd orbitals → sp³d²
  • Square planar → dsp² (always inner orbital)
  • Tetrahedral → sp³ (no choice of d-orbitals)
  • VBT explains geometry & magnetic moment
  • Limitation: Cannot explain colour or exact splitting
  • Strong field ligands force pairing → low spin
  • JEE asks: Hybridisation & magnetic moment using VBT

6. Crystal Field Theory (CFT)

  • Purely electrostatic model: Ligands as point charges
  • Octahedral splitting: t₂g (lower) & e_g (higher), Δ_o
  • Tetrahedral splitting: e (lower) & t₂ (higher), Δ_t = 4/9 Δ_o
  • Strong field ligands → large Δ_o → low spin
  • Weak field ligands → small Δ_o → high spin
  • Spectrochemical series: I⁻ < Br⁻ < Cl⁻ < F⁻ < OH⁻ < H₂O < NH₃ < en < NO₂⁻ < CN⁻ < CO
  • CFSE = (–0.4 × n_t₂g) + (0.6 × n_e_g) + P (pairing energy)
  • Jahn-Teller distortion: Unequal occupancy → distortion
  • CFT explains colour, magnetism, stability
  • JEE favourite: CFSE calculation & high/low spin

7. Colour & Magnetic Properties

  • Colour due to d–d transition (t₂g → e_g or vice versa)
  • Complementary colour absorbed → observed colour transmitted
  • d⁰ and d¹⁰ complexes colourless (no d-d transition)
  • Magnetic moment μ = √n(n+2) BM (n = unpaired electrons)
  • Spin-only formula used in JEE
  • Low spin → less unpaired → low μ
  • High spin → more unpaired → high μ
  • Square planar always diamagnetic (except rare cases)
  • Tetrahedral usually high spin
  • JEE loves: Predict colour & magnetic moment

8. Importance of Coordination Compounds

  • Qualitative analysis: Ni²⁺ → red with DMG, Fe²⁺ → blue with K₃[Fe(CN)₆]
  • Extraction: Ag, Au using cyanide process
  • Haemoglobin: Fe-porphyrin complex (O₂ carrier)
  • Chlorophyll: Mg-porphyrin (photosynthesis)
  • Vitamin B₁₂: Co-corrin complex
  • Cisplatin: Anticancer drug [Pt(NH₃)₂Cl₂]
  • EDTA: Used in hardness of water & metal poisoning
  • Wilkinson catalyst: [(Ph₃P)₃RhCl] for hydrogenation
  • Zeise’s salt: First organometallic compound
  • JEE asks: Biological role & applications
