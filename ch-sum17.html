Quick Revision Points

1. Alcohols (ROH)

  • General methods of preparation: Hydration of alkenes, hydrolysis of alkyl halides, reduction of carbonyl compounds, Grignard + carbonyl (except formaldehyde → 1° alcohol)
  • Properties: Higher boiling point than alkanes (H-bonding), soluble in water (H-bonding), acidic nature (1° > 2° > 3°)
  • Identification: Lucas test (3° > 2° > 1°), iodoform test for CH3–CH(OH)–
  • Dehydration mechanism: E1 for 3° & 2°, E2 for 1°; follows Zaitsev rule; carbocation rearrangement possible
  • Reaction with HX: 3° > 2° > 1° (SN1 vs SN2); order of reactivity HI > HBr > HCl
  • Oxidation: 1° → aldehyde → acid, 2° → ketone, 3° no oxidation; PCC stops at aldehyde
  • Esterification with carboxylic acids (Fischer); used in perfumes, flavours
  • Conversion to alkyl halides, ethers (Williamson), dehydration to alkenes
  • Uses: Ethanol (beverages, solvent, antifreeze), methanol (fuel, solvent), glycerol (cosmetics, explosives)
  • Victor Meyer test: distinguishes 1°, 2°, 3° alcohols via colour reactions

2. Phenols (ArOH)

  • General preparation: Cumene hydroperoxide, Dow’s process, from diazonium salts, alkali fusion of sulphonates
  • Acidic nature: More acidic than alcohols (resonance stabilization of phenoxide ion), less than carboxylic acids
  • Order of acidity: o-nitrophenol > p-nitrophenol > phenol > o-cresol (intramolecular H-bonding reduces acidity)
  • Electrophilic aromatic substitution: –OH is o/p director & strong activator
  • Halogenation: With Br2 water → 2,4,6-tribromophenol (white ppt)
  • Nitration: Dil HNO3 → o & p-nitrophenol; conc → 2,4,6-trinitrophenol (picric acid)
  • Sulphonation: Conc H2SO4 at 288K → o-phenolsulphonic acid, at 373K → p-isomer
  • Reimer-Tiemann: Phenol + CHCl3 + NaOH → salicylaldehyde (ortho attack)
  • Kolbe-Schmitt: Sodium phenoxide + CO2 → sodium salicylate → salicylic acid
  • Uses: Antiseptics (phenol), picric acid (explosive), salicylic acid (aspirin precursor)

3. Ethers (R–O–R')

  • General preparation: Williamson synthesis (best for unsymmetrical), alkoxide + alkyl halide
  • Properties: Low boiling, inert to most reagents, good solvent, dipole moment
  • Symmetrical ethers from conc H2SO4 + alcohol (140°C)
  • Reaction with HI: Cold HI → alkyl iodide + alcohol; excess hot HI → 2 alkyl iodides
  • Order of reactivity with HI: Alkyl aryl > diaryl (phenoxide stable)
  • If one aryl group present, only alkyl part cleaved
  • Forms oxonium salts with strong acids
  • Used as anaesthetic (diethyl ether), solvent in Grignard & Wurtz
  • Cannot undergo dehydration or oxidation easily
  • Structure: Bent, bond angle ~110°

4. Aldehydes & Ketones (>C=O)

  • Preparation: Oxidation of 1°/2° alcohols, ozonolysis, Rosenmund, Etard, Gattermann-Koch, Friedel-Crafts acylation
  • Nature: Carbonyl carbon electron deficient → nucleophilic addition
  • Reactivity: Aldehydes > ketones (steric + +I effect of two alkyl groups)
  • Addition of HCN → cyanohydrin; NH3 derivatives → imine, oxime, hydrazone, semicarbazone
  • Grignard → 2°/3° alcohol from aldehyde/ketone
  • Oxidation: Aldehyde → acid (Tollens, Fehling, KMnO4); ketone no reaction except haloform
  • Reduction: Wolff-Kishner (N2H4+KOH) & Clemmensen (Zn(Hg)/HCl) → alkane
  • Aldol condensation (α-H required), crossed aldol, Cannizzaro (no α-H), haloform (methyl carbonyl)
  • Chemical tests: Tollens (silver mirror), Fehling (red ppt), 2,4-DNP (orange ppt), iodoform (yellow ppt)
  • Uses: Formaldehyde (40% formalin, urea-formaldehyde resin), acetone (solvent), benzaldehyde (flavour)

5. Carboxylic Acids (RCOOH)

  • Preparation: Oxidation of 1° alcohol/aldehyde, Grignard + CO2, hydrolysis of nitriles, esters, acid derivatives
  • Acidic strength: Stronger than phenols & alcohols due to resonance stabilized carboxylate ion
  • Factors affecting acidity: –I groups increase, +I decrease; order: formic > acetic > propanoic
  • Electron-withdrawing groups (Cl, NO2) increase acidity; α-halo acids strongest among aliphatic
  • Reaction with bases → salts; decarboxylation (soda lime → alkane)
  • Esterification, anhydride formation, HVZ reaction (α-halogenation)
  • Reduction with LiAlH4 → 1° alcohol
  • Aromatic acids: Benzoic acid from toluene oxidation; o/p directive but deactivating in EAS
  • Uses: Acetic acid (vinegar, vinyl acetate), formic acid (coagulant), oxalic acid (cleaning), benzoic acid (preservative)
  • Hell-Volhard-Zelinsky (HVZ): α-hydrogen replaced by Br using X2 + P

6. JEE Must-Revise Points

  • Lucas: 3° immediate, 2° 5–10 min, 1° no turbidity
  • Reimer-Tiemann → salicylaldehyde; Kolbe → salicylic acid
  • Tollens & Fehling positive only for aldehydes
  • Cannizzaro: HCHO fastest; crossed Cannizzaro: HCHO oxidized preferentially
  • Acidity order: HCOOH > CH3COOH > CH3CH2COOH
  • o-nitrophenol steam volatile, p-nitrophenol high mp (H-bonding)
  • Williamson best for unsymmetrical ether
  • Aldol: 4-carbon product, self & crossed possible
  • Iodoform: CH3COR, CH3CH(OH)R, ethanol, acetaldehyde
  • Picric acid = 2,4,6-trinitrophenol (explosive)
