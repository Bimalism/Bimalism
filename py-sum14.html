1. Need of Displacement Current

  • Ampere’s law ∮ B·dl = μ₀ I was inconsistent between capacitor plates (I = 0)
  • Changing electric field produces magnetic field → need extra term
  • Maxwell introduced displacement current I_d = ε₀ dΦ_E/dt
  • Total current = conduction current + displacement current
  • Modified Ampere’s law: ∮ B·dl = μ₀ (I_c + I_d) = μ₀ (I_c + ε₀ dΦ_E/dt)
  • Displacement current exists only where E is changing with time
  • In capacitor, I_d = I (conduction current in wires)
  • Displacement current has no flow of charge, only changing E
  • Essential for continuity of magnetic field and EM wave propagation
  • Made Maxwell’s equations symmetric and complete

2. Electromagnetic Waves & Their Characteristics

  • EM waves are transverse waves produced by accelerating charges
  • E and B fields are perpendicular to each other and to direction of propagation
  • E × B gives direction of propagation (right-hand rule)
  • In free space, E₀/B₀ = c = 3 × 10⁸ m/s (speed of light)
  • Speed c = 1/√(μ₀ε₀), proves light is EM wave
  • Energy density: u_E = (1/2)ε₀E², u_B = B²/(2μ₀), average u_E = u_B
  • Poynting vector S = (1/μ₀) E × B → represents energy flow
  • Intensity I = average |S| = (1/2) c ε₀ E₀²
  • EM waves carry both energy and momentum
  • No medium required → travel in vacuum

3. Electromagnetic Spectrum

  • EM spectrum: Radio → Microwave → IR → Visible → UV → X-ray → γ-ray
  • Radio waves (> 0.1 m): produced by LC oscillations, used in communication
  • Microwaves (1 mm – 0.1 m): radar, microwave oven, 5G
  • Infrared (700 nm – 1 mm): heat radiation, remote controls
  • Visible light (400–700 nm): only part detected by human eye
  • Ultraviolet (10–400 nm): from sun, causes tanning, harmful
  • X-rays (0.01–10 nm): produced by bombarding metal, used in medical imaging
  • Gamma rays (< 0.01 nm): from radioactive decay, highly penetrating
  • All EM waves have same speed c in vacuum
  • Frequency order: γ > X-ray > UV > Visible > IR > Microwave > Radio
