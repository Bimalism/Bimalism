1. Fundamentals of Thermodynamics

  • System: Part of universe under study; Surroundings: Everything else
  • Open system: Exchange both matter and energy
  • Closed system: Exchange energy only
  • Isolated system: No exchange of matter or energy
  • Extensive properties: Depend on quantity (V, m, U, H, S, G)
  • Intensive properties: Independent of quantity (T, P, density, Cp)
  • State functions: Depend only on state (U, H, S, G, V, T, P)
  • Path functions: Depend on path (q, w)
  • Types of processes: Isothermal (ΔT=0), Adiabatic (q=0), Isochoric (ΔV=0), Isobaric (ΔP=0)
  • JEE loves identifying state vs path function & type of system

2. First Law of Thermodynamics

  • ΔU = q + w (sign convention: work done on system is +ve)
  • IUPAC: w = –PΔV (expansion work negative)
  • Internal energy (U): Total energy of system, state function
  • For ideal gas, ΔU = nCvΔT (depends only on T)
  • Work in isothermal reversible expansion: w = –nRT ln(Vf/Vi)
  • Work in irreversible expansion: w = –PextΔV
  • Maximum work → reversible process
  • Adiabatic reversible: w = nCv(T2–T1)
  • First law proves energy conservation
  • JEE asks calculation of q, w, ΔU, ΔH in different processes

3. Enthalpy & Heat Capacity

  • Enthalpy H = U + PV (state function)
  • ΔH = ΔU + Δn_g RT (for reactions with gases)
  • Heat capacity C = q/ΔT; Molar heat capacity at constant volume Cv, pressure Cp
  • Cp – Cv = R (ideal gas)
  • q_p = ΔH (isobaric process), q_v = ΔU (isochoric)
  • Enthalpy is more practical than internal energy
  • For ideal gas ΔH = nCpΔT
  • Heat capacity increases with molecular complexity
  • Ratio γ = Cp/Cv (used in adiabatic processes)
  • JEE favourite: Calculate ΔH from ΔU and vice-versa

4. Hess’s Law & Various Enthalpies

  • Hess’s law: ΔH is same irrespective of path (state function)
  • Enthalpy of formation (ΔfH): Formation of 1 mole compound from elements
  • Enthalpy of combustion (ΔcH): Heat released when 1 mole burnt in excess O₂
  • Bond dissociation enthalpy: Energy to break 1 mole of bond in gas phase
  • Atomization enthalpy: Energy to convert compound to gaseous atoms
  • ΔrH = Σ Bond enthalpies (reactants) – Σ Bond enthalpies (products)
  • Enthalpy of solution, hydration, ionization, sublimation, phase transition
  • Kirchhoff equation: ΔH₂ – ΔH₁ = ΔCp (T₂ – T₁)
  • Standard state: 1 bar, 298 K (ΔH°)
  • JEE loves Hess’s law cycle & bond enthalpy calculations

5. Second Law & Entropy

  • Second law: Total entropy of universe always increases for spontaneous process
  • Entropy (S): Measure of disorder, state function
  • ΔS_universe = ΔS_system + ΔS_surroundings ≥ 0
  • For reversible process ΔS_universe = 0
  • ΔS = q_rev / T (at constant T)
  • Standard entropy change ΔS° = ΣS°(products) – ΣS°(reactants)
  • Entropy increases: solid → liquid → gas, dissolution, expansion
  • Trouton's rule: ΔS_vap ≈ 88 J/mol·K for non-polar liquids
  • Absolute entropy can be measured (third law)
  • JEE asks predicting sign of ΔS & calculation

6. Gibbs Free Energy & Spontaneity

  • Gibbs energy G = H – TS (state function)
  • ΔG = ΔH – TΔS (criteria for spontaneity at constant T,P)
  • ΔG < 0 → spontaneous; ΔG = 0 → equilibrium; ΔG > 0 → non-spontaneous
  • ΔG_system = –TΔS_universe (links both laws)
  • For phase change: ΔG = 0 at melting/boiling point
  • Temperature dependence: High T → entropy dominant, Low T → enthalpy dominant
  • Ellingham diagram uses ΔG vs T for metallurgy
  • ΔG = w_max (maximum non-expansion work)
  • Standard Gibbs energy ΔG° = –RT ln K
  • JEE favourite: Predict spontaneity using ΔH & ΔS signs

7. Standard Gibbs Energy & Equilibrium Constant

  • ΔG° = –RT ln K (most important relation)
  • For K > 1, ΔG° < 0 (reaction goes forward)
  • ΔG° = Σ ΔfG°(products) – Σ ΔfG°(reactants)
  • At equilibrium ΔG = 0, Q = K
  • ΔG = ΔG° + RT ln Q (reaction quotient)
  • Temperature effect on K: ln(K2/K1) = –ΔH°/R (1/T2 – 1/T1) (van’t Hoff)
  • Le Chatelier’s principle from thermodynamics
  • ΔG° determines feasibility, not rate
  • Standard values at 298 K, 1 bar
  • JEE asks calculation of K from ΔG° and vice-versa

8. JEE Most Important Points (Revise Daily)

  • State vs path function: U,H,S,G → state; q,w → path
  • w_rev(isothermal) = –nRT ln(Vf/Vi), w_irrev = –PextΔV
  • ΔH = ΔU + ΔngRT
  • Hess’s law cycle & bond enthalpy calculation
  • Cp – Cv = R, γ = Cp/Cv
  • ΔG = ΔH – TΔS → spontaneity criteria
  • ΔG° = –RT ln K (memorize)
  • Sign of ΔS for phase changes, dissolution, gas evolution
  • Maximum work in reversible process
  • All four cases of ΔH & ΔS for spontaneity
