JEE Physics Chapters Summary

Key concepts and formulas for JEE exam preparation

Chapter 1

Physical World and Measurement

  • SI units: kg, m, s, A, K, mol, cd
  • Dimensional formula: [M^a L^b T^c]
  • Error propagation: Δz/z = Δx/x + Δy/y
  • Significant figures rules
  • Least count: Smallest measurable value
  • Order of magnitude estimation
  • Fundamental and derived units
  • Accuracy vs precision
  • Systematic and random errors
  • Parallax method for distance
Chapter 2

Kinematics

  • Displacement: Δx = x₂ - x₁
  • Velocity: v = dx/dt
  • Acceleration: a = dv/dt
  • Equation of motion: v = u + at
  • Distance: s = ut + ½at²
  • Velocity squared: v² = u² + 2as
  • Relative velocity: v_AB = v_A - v_B
  • Projectile range: R = (u² sin2θ)/g
  • Time of flight: T = (2u sinθ)/g
  • Maximum height: H = (u² sin²θ)/(2g)
Chapter 3

Laws of Motion

  • Newton’s 1st law: Inertia
  • Newton’s 2nd law: F = ma
  • Newton’s 3rd law: Action = -Reaction
  • Friction: f = μN
  • Impulse: J = FΔt
  • Momentum: p = mv
  • Pulley systems: Tensions
  • Free body diagrams
  • Centripetal force: F = mv²/r
  • Constraint equations
Chapter 4

Work, Energy and Power

  • Work: W = F·d cosθ
  • Kinetic energy: KE = ½mv²
  • Potential energy: PE = mgh
  • Work-energy theorem: W = ΔKE
  • Power: P = W/t
  • Conservation of energy
  • Spring energy: PE = ½kx²
  • Collision: Elastic and inelastic
  • Coefficient of restitution: e
  • Work by variable force: W = ∫F dx
Chapter 5

Rotational Motion

  • Angular velocity: ω = dθ/dt
  • Angular acceleration: α = dω/dt
  • Torque: τ = r × F
  • Moment of inertia: I = Σmr²
  • Angular momentum: L = Iω
  • Rotational KE: KE = ½Iω²
  • Parallel axis theorem
  • Perpendicular axis theorem
  • Rolling motion: v = rω
  • Conservation of angular momentum
Chapter 6

Gravitation

  • Gravitational force: F = GMm/r²
  • Gravitational potential: V = -GM/r
  • Escape velocity: v_e = √(2GM/r)
  • Orbital velocity: v_o = √(GM/r)
  • Kepler’s 1st law: Elliptical orbits
  • Kepler’s 2nd law: Equal areas
  • Kepler’s 3rd law: T² ∝ r³
  • Gravitational field: g = GM/r²
  • Potential energy: PE = -GMm/r
  • Variation of g with height, depth
Chapter 7

Properties of Solids and Liquids

  • Young’s modulus: Y = stress/strain
  • Pressure: P = F/A
  • Archimedes’ principle
  • Buoyant force: F_b = ρVg
  • Pascal’s law: Pressure transmission
  • Bernoulli’s equation: P + ρgh + ½ρv²
  • Viscosity: F = ηA(dv/dx)
  • Surface tension: T = F/l
  • Poiseuille’s formula: Q = (πPr⁴)/(8ηl)
  • Stokes’ law: F = 6πηrv
Chapter 8

Thermodynamics

  • 1st law: ΔQ = ΔU + ΔW
  • Work done: W = PΔV
  • Specific heat: Q = mcΔT
  • Ideal gas law: PV = nRT
  • 2nd law: Entropy increases
  • Carnot efficiency: η = 1 - (T₂/T₁)
  • Adiabatic process: PV^γ = constant
  • Isothermal process: PV = constant
  • Molar specific heat: C_p - C_v = R
  • Heat transfer: Conduction, convection, radiation
Chapter 9

Kinetic Theory of Gases

  • Pressure: P = (1/3)(nmv²)/V
  • RMS speed: v_rms = √(3RT/M)
  • Average KE: KE = (3/2)kT
  • Degrees of freedom
  • Law of equipartition of energy
  • Mean free path: λ = 1/(√2πd²N)
  • Avogadro’s number: N_A = 6.022×10²³
  • Boltzmann constant: k = R/N_A
  • Maxwell’s speed distribution
  • Ideal gas assumptions
Chapter 10

Oscillations and Waves

  • SHM: a = -ω²x
  • Angular frequency: ω = √(k/m)
  • Time period: T = 2π√(m/k)
  • Wave equation: v = fλ
  • Wave speed: v = √(T/μ)
  • Doppler effect: f’ = f(v±v_o)/(v±v_s)
  • Beats: f_beat = |f₁ - f₂|
  • Standing waves: λ_n = 2L/n
  • Energy in SHM: E = ½kA²
  • Superposition principle
Chapter 11

Electrostatics

  • Coulomb’s law: F = kq₁q₂/r²
  • Electric field: E = F/q
  • Gauss’s law: Φ = q/ε₀
  • Electric potential: V = kq/r
  • Capacitance: C = Q/V
  • Electric dipole moment: p = qd
  • Potential energy: U = -p·E
  • Capacitors in series: 1/C = Σ(1/C_i)
  • Capacitors in parallel: C = ΣC_i
  • Electric flux: Φ = E·A cosθ
Chapter 12

Current Electricity

  • Ohm’s law: V = IR
  • Resistance: R = ρL/A
  • Power: P = VI = I²R
  • Resistors in series: R = ΣR_i
  • Resistors in parallel: 1/R = Σ(1/R_i)
  • Kirchhoff’s 1st law: ΣI = 0
  • Kirchhoff’s 2nd law: ΣV = 0
  • Wheatstone bridge: Balanced condition
  • EMF and internal resistance
  • Drift velocity: v_d = I/(nqA)
Chapter 13

Magnetic Effects of Current

  • Biot-Savart law: dB = (μ₀I dl×r)/(4πr³)
  • Ampere’s law: ∮B·dl = μ₀I
  • Force on conductor: F = ILB sinθ
  • Lorentz force: F = q(v×B)
  • Magnetic moment: M = IA
  • Magnetic field (solenoid): B = μ₀nI
  • Cyclotron frequency: f = qB/(2πm)
  • Torque on loop: τ = M×B
  • Moving coil galvanometer
  • Magnetic field lines
Chapter 14

Electromagnetic Induction

  • Faraday’s law: ε = -dΦ/dt
  • Lenz’s law: Opposes flux change
  • Magnetic flux: Φ = BA cosθ
  • Induced EMF: ε = BLv (motional)
  • Self-inductance: ε = -L dI/dt
  • Mutual inductance: ε = -M dI/dt
  • Energy in inductor: E = ½LI²
  • Transformer: V_s/V_p = N_s/N_p
  • AC generator principle
  • Eddy currents
Chapter 15

Electromagnetic Waves

  • EM wave speed: c = 1/√(μ₀ε₀)
  • Wave equation: E = E₀ sin(ωt-kx)
  • Energy density: u = ½ε₀E²
  • Poynting vector: S = (E×B)/μ₀
  • Intensity: I = u_avg c
  • Maxwell’s equations
  • EM spectrum: Radio to gamma
  • Polarization of waves
  • Displacement current
  • Transverse nature of EM waves
Chapter 16

Optics

  • Snell’s law: n₁sinθ₁ = n₂sinθ₂
  • Lens formula: 1/f = 1/v - 1/u
  • Magnification: m = h’/h = v/u
  • Power of lens: P = 1/f (in meters)
  • Refraction: n = c/v
  • Total internal reflection: sinθ_c = n₂/n₁
  • Young’s double slit: d sinθ = nλ
  • Single slit diffraction: a sinθ = nλ
  • Optical path: μx
  • Polarization: Brewster’s angle
Chapter 17

Dual Nature of Matter

  • Photoelectric effect: E = hν
  • Work function: φ = hν₀
  • De Broglie wavelength: λ = h/p
  • Einstein’s equation: KE_max = hν - φ
  • Planck’s constant: h = 6.626×10⁻³⁴ Js
  • Momentum: p = h/λ
  • Wave-particle duality
  • Photon energy: E = hc/λ
  • Stopping potential: V_s = (hν - φ)/e
  • Davisson-Germer experiment
Chapter 18

Atoms and Nuclei

  • Bohr’s model: E_n = -13.6/n² eV
  • Radius: r_n = 0.529n²/Z Å
  • Energy difference: ΔE = hc/λ
  • Radioactive decay: N = N₀e^(-λt)
  • Half-life: T_½ = ln(2)/λ
  • Binding energy: BE = Δmc²
  • Alpha decay: A→A-4, Z→Z-2
  • Beta decay: Z→Z±1
  • Gamma decay: Energy emission
  • Nuclear fission and fusion
Chapter 19

Electronic Devices

  • Energy bands: Conduction, valence
  • p-n junction: Forward, reverse bias
  • Diode equation: I = I₀(e^(V/V_T) - 1)
  • Transistor: NPN, PNP
  • Common emitter gain: β = I_C/I_B
  • Logic gates: AND, OR, NOT, NAND, NOR
  • Zener diode: Voltage regulation
  • Photodiode: Light detection
  • LED: Light emission
  • Semiconductor doping
Chapter 20

Communication Systems

  • Bandwidth: f_max - f_min
  • Amplitude modulation: m = (V_max - V_min)/(V_max + V_min)

  • Propagation: Ground, sky, space waves
  • Antenna length: L = λ/4
  • Modulation index: μ = A_m/A_c
  • Shannon’s capacity: C = B log₂(1 + S/N)
  • Optical communication: Fiber optics
  • Satellite communication
