1. Equation of State of a Perfect Gas

  • Ideal gas obeys PV = nRT for all values of P, V, T.
  • Also written as PV = NkT (N = total molecules, k = Boltzmann constant).
  • Boyle’s law: PV = constant (at constant T).
  • Charles’s law: V/T = constant (at constant P).
  • Gay-Lussac’s law: P/T = constant (at constant V).
  • Avogadro’s law: V ∝ n (at constant P, T).
  • At STP (0°C, 1 atm), 1 mole occupies 22.4 L.
  • Universal gas constant R = 8.314 J/mol·K.
  • Boltzmann constant k = R/N_A = 1.38 × 10⁻²³ J/K.
  • Ideal gas equation is a combination of Boyle, Charles & Avogadro laws.

2. Work Done on Compressing a Gas

  • Work done by gas = ∫ P dV (area under P-V graph).
  • Work done on gas = –∫ P dV.
  • For isochoric process (V = constant): W = 0.
  • For isobaric process: W = PΔV.
  • For isothermal process (PV = constant): W = nRT ln(V_f/V_i).
  • For adiabatic process: W = (P_i V_i – P_f V_f)/(γ – 1) = nR(T_i – T_f)/(γ – 1).
  • Work is maximum in isobaric expansion (rectangular area).
  • Work is zero in isochoric process (no area).
  • In P-V diagram, clockwise loop → work done by system is positive.
  • Sign convention: Work done on system is negative in first law (modern).

3. Kinetic Theory of Gases – Assumptions

  • Gaseous molecules are point masses with negligible volume.
  • No intermolecular forces except during elastic collisions.
  • Molecules move in straight lines with constant speed between collisions.
  • Collisions with walls and among molecules are perfectly elastic.
  • Time of collision is negligible compared to time between collisions.
  • All molecules have same mass but random velocities.
  • Pressure P = (1/3) ρ v_rms² (derived from assumptions).
  • Kinetic theory explains gas laws successfully.
  • At absolute zero (0 K), molecular motion ceases.
  • Derivation leads to PV = (1/3) m N = (2/3) N (½ m ).

4. Kinetic Energy and Temperature

  • Average translational KE per molecule = (3/2) kT.
  • Total translational KE of gas = (3/2) nRT = (3/2) NkT.
  • Temperature is measure of average translational kinetic energy.
  • At same temperature, all ideal gases have same average KE per molecule.
  • Root mean square speed v_rms = √(3RT/M) = √(3kT/m).
  • Average speed = √(8kT/πm) ≈ 0.92 v_rms.
  • Most probable speed v_mp = √(2kT/m) ≈ 0.82 v_rms.
  • v_rms > > v_mp.
  • Speed ratio v_rms : : v_mp = √3 : √(8/π) : √2 ≈ 1 : 0.92 : 0.82.
  • All speeds ∝ √T and ∝ 1/√M.

5. Degrees of Freedom

  • Number of independent ways a molecule can store energy.
  • Translational degrees: 3 (along x, y, z).
  • Rotational degrees: diatomic/linear → 2, non-linear → 3.
  • Monatomic gas: f = 3 (only translational).
  • Diatomic gas (at room temp): f = 5 (3 trans + 2 rot).
  • Diatomic at very high T: f = 7 (vibrational mode activates).
  • Polyatomic linear (CO₂): f = 5 at room temp, f = 7 at high T.
  • Non-linear polyatomic: f = 6 at room temp, f = 9 at high T.
  • Average energy per degree of freedom = (1/2) kT per molecule.
  • Total internal energy U = (f/2) nRT.

6. Law of Equipartition of Energy & Specific Heat Capacity

  • Each degree of freedom contributes (1/2) kT energy per molecule.
  • For f degrees: molar internal energy U = (f/2) RT.
  • C_v = dU/dT = (f/2) R.
  • C_p = C_v + R = (f/2 + 1) R = (f+2)/2 R.
  • γ = C_p / C_v = 1 + 2/f.
  • Monatomic: f=3 → C_v = 3/2 R, γ = 5/3 ≈ 1.67.
  • Diatomic (room temp): f=5 → C_v = 5/2 R, γ = 7/5 = 1.4.
  • Diatomic (high T): f=7 → C_v = 7/2 R, γ = 9/7 ≈ 1.286.
  • Polyatomic non-linear: γ ≈ 1.33 at room temperature.
  • At absolute zero, vibrational modes freeze → C_v decreases.

7. Concept of Mean Free Path

  • Mean free path λ = average distance travelled between two successive collisions.
  • λ = 1 / (√2 π n d²), where n = number density, d = molecular diameter.
  • λ ∝ 1/P (inversely proportional to pressure).
  • λ ∝ T (directly proportional to temperature).
  • λ ∝ V (inversely proportional to density).
  • At STP, λ ≈ 10⁻⁷ m (100 nm) for air molecules.
  • In vacuum, λ becomes very large (Knudsen effect).
  • Collision frequency Z = / λ = √(8kT/πm) × (√2 π n d²).
  • Higher the temperature, higher the speed → higher collision frequency.
  • Mean free path is crucial for viscosity, diffusion and thermal conduction in gases.
