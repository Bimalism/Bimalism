1. Breathing and Respiration

  • Respiration involves intake of O₂ and removal of CO₂; breathing is the physical process.
  • Human respiratory system: nostrils → nasal chamber → pharynx → larynx → trachea → bronchi → bronchioles → alveoli.
  • Alveoli are the primary sites of gas exchange (thin, moist, highly vascular).
  • Mechanism of breathing: Inspiration (diaphragm contracts, volume ↑, pressure ↓); Expiration (diaphragm relaxes, volume ↓, pressure ↑).
  • Respiratory volumes: TV = 500 mL, IRV = 2500–3000 mL, ERV = 1000–1100 mL, RV = 1100–1200 mL.
  • Vital capacity (VC) = IRV + TV + ERV ≈ 4000–4600 mL.
  • Exchange of gases: O₂ diffuses from alveoli to blood, CO₂ from blood to alveoli (partial pressure gradient).
  • 98.5% O₂ transported as oxyhaemoglobin, 70% CO₂ as bicarbonate ions, 20–25% as carbamino-haemoglobin.
  • Respiratory rhythm controlled by respiratory centres in medulla and pons; chemoreceptors monitor CO₂, H⁺, O₂ levels.
  • Disorders: Asthma (inflammation, bronchospasm), Emphysema (alveolar wall destruction), Occupational disorders (silicosis, asbestosis).

2. Body Fluids and Circulation

  • Blood = plasma (55%) + formed elements (45%): RBC, WBC, platelets.
  • RBC contain haemoglobin (15 g/dL), life span ~120 days; ABO and Rh blood groups.
  • Blood clotting: thromboplastin → prothrombin → thrombin → fibrinogen → fibrin (mesh) + clotting factors.
  • Lymph: plasma + WBCs (no RBCs), returns proteins and fluid to blood, immunity.
  • Human heart: 4 chambers, sino-atrial node (pacemaker), double circulation (pulmonary + systemic).
  • Cardiac cycle: atrial systole (0.1 s) → ventricular systole (0.3 s) → joint diastole (0.4 s); heart sound “lub-dub”.
  • Cardiac output = stroke volume (70 mL) × heart rate (72/min) ≈ 5 L/min.
  • ECG: P wave (atrial depolarization), QRS (ventricular depolarization), T wave (ventricular repolarization).
  • Regulation: neural (sympathetic ↑, parasympathetic ↓) and hormonal (adrenaline ↑ rate & force).
  • Disorders: Hypertension (>140/90), CAD (atherosclerosis), Angina (chest pain), Heart failure (heart cannot pump enough blood).

3. Excretory Products and Their Elimination

  • Ammonotelism (NH₃ – aquatic), Ureotelism (urea – mammals), Uricotelism (uric acid – birds, reptiles).
  • Human excretory system: 2 kidneys, 2 ureters, urinary bladder, urethra.
  • Nephron: glomerulus + Bowman’s capsule + PCT + Henle’s loop + DCT + collecting duct.
  • Urine formation: glomerular filtration (GFR ~125 mL/min) → tubular reabsorption (99% water) → secretion.
  • Osmoregulation by counter-current mechanism in Henle’s loop and vasa recta (JUxta medullary nephrons).
  • Regulation: RAAS (renin → angiotensin II → vasoconstriction + aldosterone → Na⁺ reabsorption), ANF (opposite), ADH (aquaporins in CD).
  • Diabetes insipidus: ADH deficiency → dilute urine, polyuria.
  • Other organs: lungs (CO₂), liver (urea synthesis), skin (sweat), saliva.
  • Disorders: Uraemia (urea in blood), Renal calculi (stones), Renal failure, Nephritis; treated by haemodialysis.
  • Micturition: controlled by neural mechanism and stretch receptors in bladder.

4. Locomotion and Movement

  • Types of movement: amoeboid (leucocytes), ciliary (respiratory tract), muscular (limbs).
  • Skeletal muscle: multinucleated, striated, voluntary; myofibrils contain actin (thin) & myosin (thick).
  • Sliding filament theory: myosin head binds actin → power stroke → sarcomere shortens.
  • Energy from ATP; red fibres (myoglobin, aerobic), white fibres (anaerobic, fast).
  • Human skeleton: 206 bones – axial (80), appendicular (126); functions – support, protection, movement.
  • Joints: fibrous (immovable), cartilaginous (slightly movable), synovial (freely movable – ball & socket, hinge, pivot, gliding).
  • Mechanism of muscle contraction: Ca²⁺ binds troponin → tropomyosin shifts → myosin binding sites exposed.
  • Disorders: Myasthenia gravis (autoimmune, ACh receptor antibodies), Tetany (low Ca²⁺), Muscular dystrophy (genetic), Arthritis (inflammation), Osteoporosis (low bone mass), Gout (uric acid crystals).
  • Ciliary movement in fallopian tube, flagellar in spermatozoa.
  • Vertebral column has 33 vertebrae in embryo, 26 in adult (fused sacral & coccygeal).

5. Neural Control and Coordination

  • Neuron: dendrite → cell body → axon → synaptic knob; myelinated (faster conduction).
  • CNS: brain + spinal cord; PNS: cranial (12 pairs) + spinal nerves (31 pairs); Visceral: autonomic (sympathetic & parasympathetic).
  • Resting potential: –70 mV (Na⁺/K⁺ pump, K⁺ leakage).
  • Action potential: Na⁺ influx → depolarization → K⁺ efflux → repolarization; all-or-none.
  • Saltatory conduction in myelinated fibres (node of Ranvier).
  • Synapse: electrical (rare) or chemical (neurotransmitter – ACh, GABA, dopamine).
  • Reflex arc: receptor → sensory neuron → interneuron → motor neuron → effector.
  • Human brain: forebrain (cerebrum, thalamus, hypothalamus), midbrain, hindbrain (pons, cerebellum, medulla).
  • EEG records brain’s electrical activity.
  • Eye and ear are dealt separately but part of this unit in some syllabi.

6. Chemical Coordination and Integration

  • Endocrine glands: ductless, release hormones directly into blood.
  • Hypothalamus: releasing & inhibiting hormones, controls pituitary; secretes oxytocin & ADH.
  • Pituitary (master gland): anterior (GH, TSH, ACTH, FSH, LH, PRL), posterior (oxytocin, ADH).
  • Thyroid: T₃, T₄ (basal metabolism, calcitonin ↓ Ca²⁺).
  • Parathyroid: PTH ↑ blood Ca²⁺.
  • Adrenal: cortex (glucocorticoids, mineralocorticoids), medulla (adrenaline, noradrenaline).
  • Pancreas: islets – α (glucagon ↑ glucose), β (insulin ↓ glucose), δ (somatostatin).
  • Gonads: testis (testosterone), ovary (estrogen, progesterone).
  • Mechanism: steroid hormones bind intracellular receptors; peptide/protein act via second messenger (cAMP).
  • Disorders: Dwarfism & Gigantism (GH), Goitre (iodine deficiency), Diabetes mellitus (insulin), Addison’s disease (adrenal insufficiency).
