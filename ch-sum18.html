1. Amines (R–NH₂, R₂NH, R₃N)

  • Classification: 1° (R–NH₂), 2° (R₂NH), 3° (R₃N); Aliphatic & Aromatic (aniline)
  • Nomenclature: Aliphatic → alkanamine, Aromatic → aniline or N-alkyl aniline
  • General preparation: Ammonolysis of RX, Reduction of nitro/nitrile/amide, Gabriel synthesis, Hofmann bromamide
  • Structure: Pyramidal (sp³), inversion possible in aliphatic amines
  • Boiling point: 1° > 2° > 3° (H-bonding), lower than alcohols
  • Solubility: Lower amines soluble due to H-bonding with water
  • Basic nature: All amines are basic (lone pair on N), form salts with acids
  • Identification: Hinsberg test (benzenesulphonyl chloride) → 1° soluble in alkali, 2° insoluble, 3° no reaction
  • Carbylamine test: Only 1° amines + CHCl₃ + KOH → foul smell (isocyanide)
  • Uses: Methylamine (solvent), aniline (dyes), quaternary salts (detergents)

2. Basic Strength of Amines

  • In gas phase: 3° > 2° > 1° > NH₃ (+I effect of alkyl groups)
  • In aqueous solution: 2° > 3° > 1° > NH₃ (solvation + inductive effect)
  • Aliphatic amines stronger base than ammonia (alkyl groups push electrons)
  • Aromatic amines (aniline) very weak base due to resonance (lone pair delocalised)
  • pKb order: Aniline > NH₃ > aliphatic amines
  • Electron-withdrawing groups (–NO₂, –Cl) in benzene ring decrease basicity
  • Electron-donating groups (–CH₃, –OCH₃) increase basicity of aromatic amines
  • Order: p-toluidine > aniline > p-nitroaniline (basicity)
  • Guanidine (H₂N–C(=NH)–NH₂) strongest organic base
  • JEE favourite: 2° > 1° > 3° in water for aliphatic amines

3. Important Reactions of Amines

  • Alkylation: Excess RX → quaternary salt
  • Acylation: Only 1° & 2° amines + acid chloride/anhydride → amide
  • Hofmann elimination: Quaternary hydroxide → alkene (least substituted, anti-Zaitsev)
  • Reaction with HNO₂: 1° aliphatic → N₂ gas (best test), 1° aromatic → diazonium, 2° → oily nitrosoamine, 3° → salt
  • Diazotization not possible for aliphatic 1° amines (unstable)
  • Carbylamine (only 1°), Mustard oil test (CS₂ + HgCl₂ → foul smell for 1°)
  • Sulphonamide formation (Hinsberg reagent)
  • Electrophilic substitution in aniline: –NH₂ strong activator & o/p director (use acetyl protection)
  • Bromination: Aniline → 2,4,6-tribromoaniline (white ppt)
  • Coupling with diazonium → azo dyes (bright colours)

4. Diazonium Salts (ArN₂⁺ X⁻)

  • Preparation: Aniline + NaNO₂ + HCl (0–5°C) → benzenediazonium chloride
  • Most important synthetic reagent in aromatic chemistry
  • Stability: Aromatic diazonium stable below 5°C, aliphatic unstable
  • Sandmeyer: CuCl/HCl → chlorobenzene, CuBr/HBr → bromobenzene, CuCN/KCN → benzonitrile
  • Gattermann: Cu/HCl or Cu/HBr → haloarene
  • Replacement: H₃PO₂ → benzene, KI → iodobenzene, NaBF₄ → fluorobenzene (Balz-Schiemann)
  • With phenol → azo dye (orange/red), with aniline → azo compound
  • Reduction: SnCl₂/HCl or ethanol → aryl hydrazine
  • Uses: Synthesis of haloarenes, cyanides, phenols, azo dyes, biphenyl (Gomberg)
  • JEE loves: Benzenediazonium fluoroborate is stable at room temp

5. Cyanides (R–C≡N) & Isocyanides (R–N≡C)

  • Preparation: RX + KCN → RCN (SN2), RX + AgCN → RNC
  • Nitrile (RCN): C attached to N, Isocyanide (RNC): N attached to C
  • Hydrolysis: RCN → amide → acid, RNC resistant
  • Reduction: RCN → 1° amine (LiAlH4), RNC → 2° methylamine
  • Addition of Grignard: RCN → ketone (after hydrolysis), RNC → no reaction
  • Carbylamine reaction: Only 1° amines give R–N≡C
  • Foul smell characteristic of isocyanides
  • Stephen reduction: RCN + SnCl₂/HCl → aldehyde
  • Used in synthesis of amines, acids, ketones
  • JEE point: KCN gives mostly nitrile, AgCN gives isocyanide

6. JEE Must-Revise Points

  • Basicity in water: 2° > 3° > 1° > NH₃ (aliphatic)
  • Hinsberg: 1° → soluble sulphonamide, 2° → insoluble, 3° → no reaction
  • Only 1° amines give carbylamine test
  • Aniline does not give Friedel-Crafts (Lewis acid complex)
  • Sandmeyer uses Cu(I), Gattermann uses Cu powder
  • p-Nitroaniline least basic among nitroanilines
  • Diazonium salt + HBF₄ → stable fluoroborate
  • Guanidine strongest base, aniline weakest aromatic amine
  • Aliphatic 1° amine + HNO₂ → N₂ gas evolution
  • Azo dye formation: Diazonium + phenol (alkaline) → orange dye
