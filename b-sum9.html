Applications in Health & Agriculture + Ethical Issues

1. Principles and Processes of Biotechnology

  • Biotechnology = use of biology to develop technologies/products; recombinant DNA technology is core.
  • Tools: Restriction enzymes (molecular scissors), DNA ligase (molecular glue), vectors (plasmid, bacteriophage), competent host (E. coli).
  • Restriction enzymes: palindromic recognition, Type II most used (EcoRI, HindIII); produce sticky or blunt ends.
  • Steps of rDNA technology: Isolation of DNA → Fragmentation → Selection → Insertion into vector → Transformation → Selection of recombinants → Culturing.
  • Cloning vectors: ori, selectable marker (ampR, tetR), cloning site, promoter (pBR322, pUC).
  • Competent cell preparation: CaCl₂ or electroporation.
  • Selection of recombinants: Insertional inactivation (blue-white screening using lacZ), antibiotic resistance.
  • Bioreactors: large vessels (10–10000 L) for mass culture; stirred-tank most common.
  • Downstream processing: separation, purification, preservation of product.
  • PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction): denaturation → annealing → extension; Taq polymerase used.

2. Applications in Health & Agriculture + Ethical Issues

  • Human insulin: A & B chains produced in E. coli using recombinant DNA → mature insulin (Humulin – first rDNA product).
  • Gene therapy: ADA deficiency treated by introducing functional ADA gene (first clinical gene therapy 1990).
  • Vaccines: subunit (Hepatitis B), DNA vaccines, edible vaccines under research.
  • Bt crops: Bacillus thuringiensis cry genes (cryIAc, cryIIAb, cryIAb) → Bt cotton, Bt brinjal, Bt corn.
  • RNAi technology: silencing mRNA (e.g., nematode-resistant tobacco using Meloidegyne incognita).
  • Golden rice: enriched with vitamin A (β-carotene) using psy & crtI genes.
  • Transgenic animals: sheep (Tracy) producing α-1-antitrypsin, mice (super mouse), cattle for increased milk.
  • Biosafety issues: GEAC (India) regulates GMOs; Cartagena Protocol for biosafety.
  • Biopiracy: illegal use of bioresources (e.g., neem, basmati patents by foreign companies).
  • Patents: granted for Bt cotton (Monsanto), but India developed indigenous Bt varieties.
