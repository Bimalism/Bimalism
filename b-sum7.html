1. Principles of Inheritance and Variation

  • Mendel’s laws: Law of Dominance, Law of Segregation (1:2:1 genotypic, 3:1 phenotypic), Law of Independent Assortment (9:3:3:1).
  • Incomplete dominance → 1:2:1 phenotypic ratio (e.g., snapdragon pink), Co-dominance → both alleles expressed (ABO blood group).
  • Multiple alleles: ABO blood group (Iᴬ, Iᴮ, i), inheritance of blood groups, Bombay phenotype (hh).
  • Pleiotropy: single gene affects multiple traits (e.g., sickle-cell anaemia, phenylketonuria).
  • Polygenic inheritance: quantitative traits (skin colour, height), additive effect → continuous variation.
  • Chromosomal theory of inheritance (Sutton & Boveri): genes are on chromosomes; linkage & crossing over (Morgan).
  • Sex determination: Humans XX-XY (heterogametic male), Birds ZW-ZZ (heterogametic female), Honey bee – haplo-diploidy.
  • Sex-linked inheritance: Haemophilia & colour blindness (X-linked recessive), more in males.
  • Mendelian disorders: Thalassemia (α or β globin), sickle-cell, phenylketonuria (autosomal recessive).
  • Chromosomal disorders: Down syndrome (trisomy 21), Turner (45,XO), Klinefelter (47,XXY).

2. Molecular Basis of Inheritance

  • DNA as genetic material proved by Griffith (transformation), Avery, Hershey-Chase (bacteriophage) experiments.
  • DNA structure (Watson-Crick): double helix, antiparallel, A=T, G≡C, right-handed.
  • DNA packaging: nucleosome (DNA + H2A, H2B, H3, H4), solenoid → chromatin fibre → chromosome.
  • DNA replication: semi-conservative (Meselson-Stahl), bidirectional, leading & lagging strand, Okazaki fragments.
  • Central dogma: DNA → mRNA (transcription) → protein (translation); reverse transcription in retroviruses.
  • Genetic code: triplet, degenerate, universal, comma-less, start (AUG), stop (UAA, UAG, UGA).
  • Lac operon (Jacob & Monod): inducible, repressor, operator, promoter, structural genes (z, y, a).
  • Human Genome Project (2003): 3.16 billion bp, ~20,500 genes; DNA fingerprinting (VNTR, Alec Jeffreys).
  • RNA types: mRNA, tRNA (clover-leaf), rRNA; protein synthesis on ribosome.
  • Regulation: operon model, promoter, operator, inducer (lactose), CAP-cAMP positive regulation.

3. Evolution

  • Origin of life: Oparin-Haldane theory, Miller-Urey experiment (amino acids from CH₄, NH₃, H₂, H₂O).
  • Evidences: Paleontology (fossils), comparative anatomy (homologous & analogous organs), embryology (ontogeny repeats phylogeny), molecular (DNA similarity).
  • Darwin’s theory: overproduction, struggle, variation, survival of fittest, natural selection.
  • Modern synthetic theory: gene pool, gene frequency, mutation, recombination, genetic drift, natural selection, isolation.
  • Hardy-Weinberg equilibrium: p² + 2pq + q² = 1; p + q = 1; disturbed by mutation, selection, migration, drift.
  • Types of natural selection: stabilizing (human birth weight), directional (DDT resistance), disruptive.
  • Genetic drift: founder effect, bottleneck effect; gene flow migration.
  • Adaptive radiation: Darwin’s finches, Australian marsupials from common ancestor.
  • Human evolution: Dryopithecus → Ramapithecus → Australopithecus → Homo habilis → H. erectus → H. sapiens.
  • Branching descent & natural selection are key mechanisms of evolution.
