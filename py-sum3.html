1. Newton’s First Law of Motion

Statement

Every body continues to be in its state of rest or of uniform motion in a straight line unless compelled by an external unbalanced force to change that state.

Law of Inertia → Inertia is the tendency of a body to oppose change in its state of motion.
Type of InertiaExample
Inertia of RestDust falls off when a carpet is beaten
Inertia of MotionPassenger jerks forward when bus stops suddenly
Inertia of DirectionMud flies off tangentially from a spinning wheel

Inertial vs Non-Inertial Frames

Inertial: Newton’s laws hold (constant velocity or rest)
Non-inertial: Accelerating → Pseudo force = −m a_frame

2. Newton’s Second Law of Motion

Statement

The rate of change of linear momentum is directly proportional to the applied force and takes place in the direction of force.

F⃗ = dp⃗/dt
If m constant → F⃗ = m a⃗
1 Newton = 1 kg·m/s²

Important Applications

  • Impulse: J = Δp = FΔt
  • Variable mass system (Rocket): F_ext + v_rel(dm/dt) = m dv/dt
  • Rocket velocity: v = v₀ + v_rel ln(m₀/m)

3. Newton’s Third Law of Motion

Statement

To every action, there is always an equal and opposite reaction.

F⃗_AB = −F⃗_BA

Key Points

  • Action and reaction act on different bodies
  • They never cancel each other
  • Examples: Recoil of gun, Swimming, Rocket propulsion, Walking

4. Law of Conservation of Linear Momentum

If net external force on a system is zero, then its total linear momentum remains constant.
m₁u₁ + m₂u₂ = m₁v₁ + m₂v₂
(For two bodies in collision)
TypeConditionMomentumKinetic Energy
Elastice = 1ConservedConserved
Inelastice < 1ConservedNot conserved
Perfectly Inelastice = 0 (stick together)ConservedMaximum loss

Coefficient of Restitution

e = (v₂ − v₁)/(u₁ − u₂) = Relative speed after / Relative speed before

6. Centripetal Force & Dynamics of Uniform Circular Motion

Centripetal Force: F_c = mv²/r = mω²r = m(4π²r)/T²
Direction: Always towards centre

Important Cases

SituationFormula
Banked Road (no friction)tan θ = v²/rg
Banked Road (with friction)v_max = √[rg (μ + tanθ)/(1 − μ tanθ)]
Conical PendulumT cosθ = mg, T sinθ = mv²/r → cosθ = g/(ω²r)
Vertical Circle (top)T + mg = mv²/r → v_min = √gr
Vertical Circle (bottom)T − mg = mv²/r → T_max = mg + mv²/r
To complete loopv_bottom ≥ √(5gr)

7. Equilibrium and Concurrent Forces

A body is in equilibrium if ΣF = 0 and Στ = 0

Types of Equilibrium

TypeConditionExample
StableCOM lowers on displacementBall in a bowl
UnstableCOM risesBall on a hill
NeutralCOM neither rises nor lowersBall on plane surface
Lami’s Theorem (for 3 concurrent forces in equilibrium):
F₁/sinα = F₂/sinβ = F₃/sinγ

Quick Revision – Must Remember

  • F = ma | Impulse = Δp
  • Action ≠ Reaction on same body
  • Momentum conserved only if F_ext = 0
  • e = 1 → Elastic | e = 0 → Perfectly inelastic
  • f_max = μ_s N > f_k = μ_k N
  • Centripetal force is NOT a new force
  • Pseudo force = −m a_frame
  • v_min at top of loop = √gr | v_min at bottom = √(5gr)
  • tanθ = v²/rg (banking)
  • Rocket: v = v_rel ln(m₀/m)
