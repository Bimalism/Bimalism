Chemistry Summary - Key Concepts for NEET Exam

1. Some Basic Concepts of Chemistry

Fundamental concepts including matter classification, laws of chemical combination, mole concept, and stoichiometry.

Key Concepts

  • Matter and its classification
  • Laws of chemical combination
  • Dalton's atomic theory
  • Mole concept and Avogadro's number

Stoichiometry

Calculations based on balanced chemical equations, limiting reagents, and percentage yield.

Mole: n = mass / molar mass

Molarity: M = moles / volume (L)

2. Structure of Atom

Atomic models, quantum numbers, electronic configuration, and spectral lines.

Atomic Models

Bohr's model, quantum mechanical model, and dual nature of matter.

Energy: E_n = -13.6 / n² eV

De Broglie wavelength: λ = h / p

Quantum Numbers

Principal (n), azimuthal (l), magnetic (m_l), spin (m_s).

3. Classification of Elements and Periodicity

Periodic table, trends in properties like atomic radius, ionization energy, and electronegativity.

Periodic Trends

  • Atomic radius decreases across period
  • Ionization energy increases across period
  • Electronegativity increases across period

s, p, d, f Blocks

Classification based on electron configuration.

4. Chemical Bonding and Molecular Structure

Ionic, covalent bonds, VSEPR theory, hybridization, and molecular orbital theory.

Bond order = (N_b - N_a) / 2

VSEPR Theory

Predicts molecular geometry based on electron pairs.

5. States of Matter

Gases, liquids, solids; gas laws, kinetic theory, and intermolecular forces.

Gas Laws

  • Boyle's law: PV = constant
  • Charles's law: V/T = constant
  • Ideal gas equation: PV = nRT

Van der Waals equation: (P + a/V²)(V - b) = RT

6. Thermodynamics

Laws of thermodynamics, enthalpy, entropy, and Gibbs free energy.

First law: ΔU = q + w

Gibbs free energy: ΔG = ΔH - TΔS

Laws

  • First law: Conservation of energy
  • Second law: Entropy increases

7. Equilibrium

Chemical equilibrium, Le Chatelier's principle, acids-bases, and solubility.

Equilibrium Constant

K_c = [products] / [reactants]

pH = -log[H⁺]

8. Redox Reactions

Oxidation-reduction, balancing equations, and electrochemical cells.

Concepts

  • Oxidation number
  • Electrode potential

Nernst equation: E = E° - (RT/nF) ln Q

9. Hydrogen

Position in periodic table, isotopes, hydrides, and water properties.

H₂O₂ decomposition: 2H₂O₂ → 2H₂O + O₂

10. The s-Block Elements

Alkali and alkaline earth metals, properties, and compounds.

Properties

  • Low ionization energy
  • High reactivity
