1. Electromagnetic Induction

  • Production of induced emf due to change in magnetic flux
  • Discovered by Faraday (1831) & Henry
  • Occurs when Φ_B through a circuit changes
  • Flux change: motion of magnet/coil or changing current
  • Induced emf exists only while flux is changing
  • Closed loop required for induced current
  • Two types: self-induction and mutual induction
  • Basis of generators, transformers, inductors
  • No contact needed between source and circuit
  • Foundation of AC technology

2. Faraday’s Laws

  • First law: Changing magnetic flux induces emf
  • Second law: ε = – dΦ_B/dt
  • For N turns: ε = –N dΦ/dt
  • Magnetic flux Φ_B = B⋅A = B A cosθ
  • Unit of flux: weber (Wb), 1 Wb = 1 T m²
  • 1 volt = 1 Wb/s
  • Negative sign shows opposition (Lenz’s law)
  • Flux change due to B, A, or θ variation
  • Motional emf: ε = B l v (rod moving ⊥ B)
  • Faraday’s law is fundamental EMI law

3. Lenz’s Law

  • Induced current opposes the cause producing it
  • Conservation of energy statement
  • Gives direction of induced emf/current
  • Negative sign in Faraday’s law represents Lenz
  • Magnet approaching coil → current repels magnet
  • Magnet receding → current attracts magnet
  • Prevents perpetual motion
  • Explains electromagnetic braking
  • Always opposes change in flux
  • Combined with Faraday gives complete law

4. Eddy Currents

  • Circulating currents in conductor due to changing B
  • Flow in closed loops ⊥ to magnetic field
  • Cause heating (I²R loss)
  • Reduced by laminating core with insulation
  • Useful in induction furnace, electromagnetic damping
  • Used in dead-beat galvanometer, metal detectors
  • Energy loss in transformer minimized by lamination
  • Direction opposes flux change (Lenz)
  • Also called Foucault currents
  • Stronger in solid core than laminated

5. Self and Mutual Inductance

  • Self inductance L = Φ/I, unit henry (H)
  • Back emf ε = –L dI/dt
  • For solenoid: L = μ₀ N² A / l
  • Energy stored = (1/2) L I²
  • Mutual inductance M = Φ₂₁/I₁
  • Induced emf ε₂ = –M dI₁/dt
  • M = μ₀ N₁ N₂ A / l (coaxial solenoids)
  • M ≤ √(L₁ L₂), max when coupling coefficient k=1
  • Inductor opposes sudden current change
  • Time constant τ = L/R in RL circuit

6. AC, Peak/RMS, Reactance & Impedance

  • i = I₀ sin(ωt + φ), v = V₀ sin(ωt + φ)
  • RMS value: I_rms = I₀/√2 ≈ 0.707 I₀
  • Peak value I₀, domestic 220 V is RMS
  • Inductive reactance X_L = ωL
  • Capacitive reactance X_C = 1/(ωC)
  • Impedance Z = √(R² + (X_L – X_C)²)
  • Current lags voltage by 90° in pure L
  • Current leads voltage by 90° in pure C
  • Phase angle φ = tan⁻¹((X_L – X_C)/R)
  • Power factor cosφ = R/Z

7. LC Oscillations

  • Energy oscillates between L and C
  • Frequency f = 1/(2π√(LC))
  • Angular frequency ω = 1/√(LC)
  • Charge q = Q₀ cos(ωt + φ)
  • Current i = –ω Q₀ sin(ωt + φ)
  • Total energy constant = (1/2)Q₀²/C
  • Ideal (no R) → undamped oscillations
  • Analogous to mechanical SHM
  • Used in radio tuning circuits
  • Resonance frequency of tank circuit

8. LCR Series Circuit

  • Impedance Z = √(R² + (X_L – X_C)²)
  • Current I = V/Z
  • Voltage drops: V_R in phase, V_L leads 90°, V_C lags 90°
  • Phasor diagram: V_R, V_L, V_C, V vector sum
  • Power P = V I cosφ = I² R
  • Average power zero in pure L or C
  • Power factor = cosφ = R/Z
  • At resonance Z = R minimum → I maximum
  • V_L and V_C can be > V (voltage magnification)
  • Wattless current in pure L/C

9. Resonance in LCR

  • Occurs when X_L = X_C → Z = R (minimum)
  • Resonant frequency ω₀ = 1/√(LC)
  • f₀ = 1/(2π√(LC))
  • Current maximum, power factor = 1
  • V_L = V_C, 180° out of phase
  • Quality factor Q = ω₀L/R = 1/(ω₀CR)
  • Higher Q → sharper resonance
  • Bandwidth = R/(2πL) = f₀/Q
  • Used in radio/TV tuning
  • Voltage amplification Q times at resonance

10. AC Generator & Transformer

  • AC generator: ε = N B A ω sinωt
  • Slip rings give AC output
  • Transformer works on mutual induction
  • V_s/V_p = N_s/N_p = I_p/I_s = k
  • Step-up: N_s > N_p → V_s > V_p
  • Step-down: N_s < N_p
  • Ideal efficiency 100%, P_s = P_p
  • Core laminated to reduce eddy loss
  • Copper loss I²R, hysteresis loss
  • High voltage transmission reduces I²R loss
