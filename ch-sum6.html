1. Concept of Dynamic Equilibrium

  • Equilibrium → rate of forward reaction = rate of backward reaction
  • Dynamic in nature → reactions never stop
  • Attained only in closed system
  • Macroscopic properties (P, T, conc) become constant
  • Can be achieved from either side
  • Catalyst does not affect position, only speeds attainment
  • Equilibrium constant is temperature dependent
  • Reversible reactions → ⇌, Irreversible → →
  • Equilibrium state ≠ static state (molecules still react)
  • JEE asks: Why ice ⇌ water is dynamic even at 0°C?

2. Physical Equilibrium & Henry’s Law

  • Solid ⇌ Liquid: Melting point (at 1 atm)
  • Liquid ⇌ Gas: Vapour pressure becomes constant
  • Henry’s law: P_gas = K_H × x_gas (solubility of gas)
  • K_H ↑ as T ↑ → solubility of gas ↓ with temperature
  • Soda bottle: High pressure → more CO₂ dissolved
  • Deep sea divers: N₂ dissolves more at high P → decompression sickness
  • Solid in liquid: Saturated solution → dynamic equilibrium
  • λ = vapour density at equilibrium
  • General: All phase changes are physical equilibrium
  • JEE loves: Why do fish die in hot water? (Less O₂)

3. Chemical Equilibrium & Equilibrium Constants

  • Law of mass action: Rate ∝ [reactants]^stoich
  • Kc = [C]^c [D]^d / [A]^a [B]^b
  • Kp = (P_C^c P_D^d) / (P_A^a P_B^b)
  • Kp = Kc (RT)^Δn_g → Δn_g = moles products – moles reactants gases
  • Kc has units if Δn ≠ 0, Kp always has units
  • If K > 10³ → product favoured, K < 10⁻³ → reactant favoured
  • Reaction quotient Q: If Q < K → forward, Q > K → backward
  • For reverse reaction K’ = 1/K
  • For multiplied reaction K’’ = K^n
  • JEE favourite: Calculate Kp from Kc and vice-versa
ΔG° = –RT ln K → K = e^(–ΔG°/RT)

4. Le Chatelier’s Principle & Factors

  • System opposes any change in T, P, conc
  • Increase conc of reactant → shifts forward
  • Increase pressure → shifts to lesser moles of gas
  • Exothermic reaction → increase T → shifts backward
  • Catalyst → no effect on position
  • Inert gas at constant volume → no effect
  • Inert gas at constant pressure → shifts to more moles
  • Removal of product → shifts forward (industrial importance)
  • Haber's process: High P, low T, remove NH₃
  • JEE asks: Predict shift in 2SO₂ + O₂ ⇌ 2SO₃

5. Ionic Equilibrium & Electrolytes

  • Strong electrolytes: Complete ionization (HCl, NaOH)
  • Weak electrolytes: Partial (CH₃COOH, NH₄OH)
  • Ostwald dilution law: K_a = α²C / (1–α) for weak acid
  • Degree of dissociation α ↑ with dilution
  • pH = –log[H⁺], pOH = –log[OH⁻]
  • pK_a = –log K_a → lower pK_a = stronger acid
  • Kw = [H⁺][OH⁻] = 10⁻¹⁴ at 298 K
  • pH + pOH = 14
  • Common ion effect: Suppresses dissociation
  • JEE loves: Calculate pH of 0.1 M CH₃COOH + 0.1 M CH₃COONa

6. Acids & Bases Concepts

  • Arrhenius: Acid → H⁺, Base → OH⁻ in water
  • Brønsted-Lowry: Acid → proton donor, Base → proton acceptor
  • Lewis: Acid → e⁻ pair acceptor, Base → e⁻ pair donor
  • Conjugate acid-base pair differ by one H⁺
  • H₂O is amphoteric
  • Strength: HCl > H₂SO₄ > HNO₃ > HF (F⁻ strong conjugate)
  • Levelling effect: All strong acids same strength in water
  • Polyprotic acids: H₃PO₄ → multistage ionization
  • pH of salts depends on hydrolysis
  • JEE asks: Which is strongest Lewis acid? BF₃

7. Buffer Solutions

  • Resist change in pH on adding small acid/base
  • Acidic buffer: Weak acid + its salt (CH₃COOH + CH₃COONa)
  • Basic buffer: Weak base + its salt (NH₄OH + NH₄Cl)
  • Henderson equation: pH = pK_a + log([salt]/[acid])
  • pOH = pK_b + log([salt]/[base])
  • Buffer capacity maximum when [acid] = [salt]
  • Blood buffer: H₂CO₃ / HCO₃⁻ (pH ~7.4)
  • Solubility of salt affected by common ion
  • Buffer works best in pK_a ± 1 range
  • JEE favourite: Calculate pH after adding HCl to buffer

8. Solubility Product (Ksp)

  • Ksp = [M⁺]^m [A⁻]^n for MxAy ⇌ mM⁺ + nA⁻
  • If ionic product > Ksp → precipitation
  • Ksp ↑ with temperature for most salts
  • Common ion decreases solubility
  • pH affects solubility of salts of weak acids (CaF₂, Ag₂CrO₄)
  • Selective precipitation: AgCl precipitates before AgBr
  • Simultaneous solubility when two salts have common ion
  • Ksp of AgCl = 1.8 × 10⁻¹⁰, CaF₂ = 3.9 × 10⁻¹¹
  • Complex formation increases solubility (AgCl in NH₃)
  • JEE asks: Minimum [OH⁻] to precipitate Mg(OH)₂

9. JEE Most Important Points (Revise Daily)

  • Kp = Kc(RT)^Δn_g → Δn_g = gaseous products – reactants
  • ΔG° = –RT ln K → K > 1 if ΔG° < 0
  • Le Chatelier: High P → less moles, High T → endothermic
  • pH = pK_a + log([salt]/[acid]) → Buffer equation
  • Ksp > Ionic product → unsaturated, = saturated, < precipitation
  • Common ion effect suppresses ionization/solubility
  • α = √(K_a/C) for weak acid (Ostwald)
  • pH of salt: NH₄Cl acidic, Na₂CO₃ basic, CH₃COONa basic
  • Henry’s law: Solubility ∝ Pressure
  • Catalyst does NOT affect K or position of equilibrium
