1. Work Done & Work-Energy Theorem

Work done by a force is the dot product of force and displacement.
W = F⃗ · s⃗ = F s cosθ

Work by Constant Force

θW = Fs cosθNature
0°+ve (max)Force helps motion
90°0e.g., mg in horizontal motion
180°−ve (max)Opposes motion
Work-Energy Theorem:
W_net = ΔK = K_f − K_i = ½mv² − ½mu²

Key Result

Net work done = Change in kinetic energy
Valid for constant or variable force, any path

2. Power

Instantaneous Power: P = F⃗ · v⃗ = F v cosθ
Average Power: P_avg = Total Work / Time
Unit: Watt = J/s
CasePower
Force ⊥ velocityP = 0 (e.g., centripetal force)
Lifting mass verticallyP = mg v
Car on level roadP = F_drive × v

3. Potential Energy

Energy stored in a body due to its position or configuration.
U = −W_conservative (from reference point)
